There are maps of different countries in the world which are smaller or larger in their size or same shape or not. If you ask yourself whether a country's different maps are like or unlike, equal in size or not, you may answer it by looking at the maps. In geometry, the concept of equality in size and shape is described as congruency of triangles and likeness is called "similarity of figures". In this unit, you will learn both congruency and similarity of triangles.

What is "congruency"? It means that one shape can become another using turns, flips and/or sliders. The equal sides and angles may not be on the same position (if there is a turn or a flip), but they are there. Congruent means to be identical in size and shape.

### Definition 7.1

When two triangles have exactly the same three sides and the same three angles they are called congruent triangles.

**Note:** Same three sides means the corresponding sides have equal length and same three angles means the corresponding angles have equal measure.

Note that ΔABC ≅ ΔDEF means AB ≅ DE, BC ≅ EF, AC ≅ DF and ∠A ≅ ∠D, ∠B ≅ ∠E, ∠C ≅ ∠F.

### Definition 7.2

Two plane figures are similar if their corresponding angles are congruent and the ratios of their corresponding sides are proportional. This common ratio is called the scale factor.

Two plane figures are similar means that:

- All corresponding angles are congruent
- All corresponding sides are proportional

When we magnify or diminish similar figures, one exactly places on the other and vice versa.

1. If the two triangles, the two rectangles and the two pentagons below satisfy the above properties then they are similar to each other.

Note that if ΔABC and ΔDEF are similar, then we write mathematically as ΔABC ~ ΔDEF.

2. Any two circles (of any radii) have the same shape and hence they are always similar. Similarly, two squares (of any side lengths) are similar.

### 7.3.1 AA Similarity Theorem

The AA similarity theorem for triangles states that if the two angles of one triangle are respectively congruent to the two angles of the other, then the triangles are similar. In short, equiangular triangles are similar. Ideally, the name of this criterion should then be the AAA (Angle-Angle-Angle) similarity theorem, but we call it as AA similarity theorem because we need only two pairs of angles to be equal - the third pair will then automatically be equal by the angle sum property of triangles. The notation ~ is used to denote similarity.

Consider the following figures, in which ΔABC and ΔDEF are equiangular.

**Figure: Equiangular Triangles**  
Two triangles with angles 65°, 60°, 55° each.

Using the AA Similarity theorem, we can say that these triangles are similar. Mathematically, it is written as ΔABC ~ ΔDEF.

#### Example

**Figure: Triangles EFG and LMN**  
Triangle EFG has angles 102°, 30°, 48°. Triangle LMN has angles 102°, unknown angles.

**Solution:**
Compare the angles to see if we can use the AA similarity theorem. Using triangle angle sum theorem, m(∠G) = 48° & m(∠M) = 30°. Hence, ΔEFG ~ ΔLMN by AA similarity theorem.

### 7.3.2 SSS Similarity Theorem

If the corresponding three sides of two triangles are proportional to each other, then the triangles are similar. This essentially means that any such pair of triangles are equiangular (all corresponding angle pairs are congruent).

#### Example 1

Determine whether the following pairs of triangles are similar by SSS similarity theorem.

**Figure: Triangles ABC and DEF**  
Triangle ABC has sides 14cm, 10cm, 12cm. Triangle DEF has sides 7cm, 5cm, 6cm.

**Solution:**
```
AB/DE = 14/7 = 2
BC/EF = 10/5 = 2
AC/DF = 12/6 = 2
```

Hence, ΔABC ~ ΔDEF by SSS similarity theorem.

#### Example 2

**Solution:**

**Figure: Triangles with Color-Coded Corresponding Sides**  
One triangle with sides 28, 33, 20. Another triangle with sides 21, 24, 15. Corresponding sides are color-coded showing 28/21 = 33/24 = 20/15 = 4/3.

Since 28/21 = 33/24 = 20/15 = 4/3, the same color are the corresponding sides. Therefore, the triangles are similar by SSS similarity theorem.

If the corresponding two sides of two triangles are proportional and the included angles are congruent, then the two triangles are similar.

#### Example 1

```
AB/DE = 12/6 = 2
AC/DF = 14/7 = 2
```

Then the two corresponding sides are in the same ratio and ∠A ≅ ∠D. Hence, ΔABC ~ ΔDEF by SAS similarity theorem.

### Definition 7.3

If two figures are similar, then the ratio of their perimeters is equal to ratio of their corresponding side lengths.

### Example 1

Assume that ΔABC ~ ΔDEF and the ratio of the lengths of their sides is 2. Then, find the ratio of the corresponding perimeters. The perimeter of ΔABC is 6+8+10 and the perimeter of ΔDEF is 3+4+5.

**Figure: Similar Triangles ABC and DEF**  
Triangle ABC with sides 6, 8, 10. Triangle DEF with sides 3, 4, 5.

**Solution:**
The perimeter of ΔABC = 6+8+10 = 24  
The perimeter of ΔDEF = 3+4+5 = 12  
Perimeter of ΔABC / Perimeter of ΔDEF = 24/12 = 2

This is equal to the ratio of the corresponding sides (AB/DE = BC/EF = CA/FD).

**Note:** AB/DE is the same as AB:DE. Both refer to the ratio of AB to DE.

### Example 2

**Solution:**
```
Perimeter of the Purple rectangle / Perimeter of the Blue rectangle = 4cm/6cm = 2/3
```

### Definition

If two plane figures are similar, then the ratio of their areas is equal to the square of the ratio of their corresponding side lengths.

### Example 1

Consider a pair of similar rectangles 1cm by 2cm and 3cm by 6cm below. The ratio of the corresponding sides is 3. Find the ratio of their areas.

**Figure: Similar Rectangles**  
Smaller rectangle: 1cm × 2cm. Larger rectangle: 3cm × 6cm.

**Solution:**
Area of the smaller rectangle = 1 × 2 = 2 cm²  
Area of the larger rectangle = 3 × 6 = 18 cm²  
The ratio of area = Area of larger rectangle / Area of smaller rectangle = 18/2 = 9 = 3² = (the ratio of the length of their corresponding side)²

In other words, since the ratio of length = 3:1, then ratio of area = 3²:1² = 9:1

### Example 2

If ΔABC and ΔDEF are similar as indicated below. Find the ratio of the area of the larger triangle to the smaller triangle.

**Figure: Similar Triangles with Corresponding Sides**  
Two similar triangles where corresponding sides are in ratio 10:6.

**Solution:**
The ratio of area = Area of the larger triangle / Area of the smaller triangle
```
= (10/6)² = 25/9
```
Therefore, the ratio of the areas is 25/9.

## 7.7 Applications of Similarity

### Activity 7.7

Given a ladder 5m long lying on a wall. If the height of the wall to the top of the ladder is 3m. How far is the bottom of the ladder from the wall?

Similar triangles are used to work out the heights of tall objects such as trees, buildings and towers without climbing, which are difficult to measure for us.

### Example 1

Almaz is 1.6 meters tall and is standing outside next to her younger brother. She notices that she can see both of their shadows and decides to measure each shadow. Her shadow is 2 meters long and her brother's shadow is 1.5 meters long. How tall is Almaz's brother?

**Solution:**
Let x be the height of the younger brother. Using proportionality of corresponding heights and length of shadows, we have:
```
1.5/2 = x/1.6
x = 1.2 meters
```
Therefore, Almaz's brother is 1.2m tall.

### Example 2

Abebe's boat has come untied and floated away on Lake Tana. He is standing on the top of a building that is 35m above the water from the lake. If he stands 10m from the edge of the building, he can visually align the top of the building with the water at the back of his boat. His eye level is 1.8m above the top of the building. Approximately, how far is Abebe's boat from the building?

**Solution:**
Let x be the distance from the boat to the building. Using proportionality of corresponding sides of the two triangles, we have:
```
35/1.8 = x/10
x ≈ 194.4m
```
Therefore, the boat is approximately 194.4m from the building.

### Example 3

Suppose BA, CF and DE be the heights of three buildings. Let DC = 1, BC = 3, CF = 4 and AB = 2 as indicated in the figure below. Find the height x of the tallest building.

**Solution:**
Extend FA and CB so that they meet at G. Now, we have ∆DGE ~ ∆CGF by AA similarity theorem.

```
(GB + 4)/(GB + 3) = x/4
```

Now, consider similar triangles AGB and FGC. From this, we get:
```
GB/(GB + 3) = AB/FC = 2/4 = 1/2
```

Solving: 2 × GB = GB + 3 → GB = 3

Therefore, x = (7/6) × 4 = 14/3

### Example 4

It is done as follows: Person A moves 15m to the left and person B moves 60m to the right. Moreover, person A moves 5m away from the river. The line connecting the places after the two people have moved passes through point A.

**Solution:**

Similar triangles ADE and AFC are formed. Therefore:

## Summary

1. Two plane figures are similar if:
   i. their corresponding sides are proportional.
   ii. their corresponding angles are equal.

2. Under an enlargement:
   a. Lines and their images are parallel.
   b. Angles remain the same.
   c. All lengths are increased or decreased in the same ratio.

3. **Scale factor:** the ratio of corresponding sides usually expressed numerically so that:
   Scale factor = length of the line segment on the enlargement / length of the line segment on the original

4. **AA Similarity theorem:** If two angles of one triangle are congruent to the corresponding two angles of another triangle, then the two triangles are similar.

5. **SSS Similarity theorem:** If the corresponding three sides of two triangles are proportional to each other, then the triangles are similar.

6. **SAS Similarity theorem:** If the corresponding two sides of two triangles are proportional and the included angles are congruent, then the two triangles are similar.

7. If two figures are similar, then the ratio of their perimeters is equal to ratio of their corresponding side lengths.

8. If two figures are similar, then the ratio of their areas is equal to the square of the ratio of their corresponding side lengths.
