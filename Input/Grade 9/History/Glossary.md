# Glossary

**Abbaa**: father, master of a ceremony/religion.

**Abbaa Gadaa**: Prime Councilor, Master of Gadaa period, Gadaa leader.

**Abbaa Muudaa**: father of anointing.

**Abba Duulaa**: commander in chief.

**Balabbat**: hereditary owner of rist land; or is an appointee at district level by the state to serve as subordinate land administrator or local land lord.

**Balambaras**: head of the amba'a low-level administrative title.

**Butta Ceremony**: the ceremony occurred every eight years by the Oromo, when power transferred from one Gadaa grade to the next.

**Chaffe**: the Oromo Gadaa assembly.

**Dejjazmach**: Politico-military title literally meaning commander of the gate.

**Etege**: equivalent to Queen.

**Fitawurari**: Politico-military title literally meaning commander of the vanguard.

**Gabbar**: tribute paying peasants either in kind or money.

**Gadaa**: an Oromo indigenous, traditional socio-political system through which the Oromo society exercise self-governance.

**Geber**: agrarian tribute, invariably paid in kind, tax.

**Gult**: non hereditary right to collect tribute given to officials.

**Guddifacha**: a form of parent adopting a child Oromo family.

**Grazmach**: Politico-military title literally meaning commander of the left.

**Jeberti**: the Ethiopian Muslim merchants of the 19th and early 20th centuries.

**Janissaries**: a member of the Turkish infantry forming the Sultan's guard between the 14th and 19th centuries.

**Kawo**: Title for King of Wolaita, Gamo and other Omotic people.

**Luwa**: is an age grade system of Sidama where each grade rotates every 8 years.

**Manor**: Villages during the medieval European period.

**Moggasa**: was a system of adopting non-Oromo community into Oromo clan.

**Odaa**: a sycamore tree chosen for the performance of religious rituals as well as political actions.

**Negadras**: head of merchant, later chief government official in charge of the collection of customs.

**Nigus**: King.

**Qaalluu**: Oromo religious leader. He leads ritual performances and prayers, anoint the participants as a religious piety. His clan and he are believed to be the 'eldest'. He is seen as a custodian of the waqeffanna and the guardian of the harmonious functioning of the Gadaa system.

**Qegnazmach**: Politico-military title literally meaning commander of the right.

**Ras**: 'head', the highest traditional title next to Emperor.

**Rist**: a lineage system of land ownership, giving usufruct right to the claimant; in the 20th century, rist assumes the meaning of absolute private property.

**Tato**: title for King of Kafa and Yem.

**Sera**: a set of cultural norms regulating the social structure of the society.

**Woma**: Title for King among Kambata, Sidama, Tambaro, Halaba and others.
