There are four primary categories of food safety hazards to consider: biological, chemical, physical, and allergenic. Understanding the risks associated with each can reduce the potential of a foodborne illness in the food chain. ## 16.2. Source of food contaminants

Major contamination sources are water, air, dust, equipment, sewage, insects, rodents, and employees. Contamination of raw materials can also occur from the soil, live animals, external surface, and the internal organs of meat animals. Additional contamination of animal foods originates from diseased animals, although advances in health care have nearly eliminated this source.
