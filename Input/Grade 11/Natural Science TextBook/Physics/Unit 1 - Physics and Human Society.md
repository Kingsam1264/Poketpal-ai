### Experimental Knowledge

**I. Sensory perception:** Sensory perception is perhaps the dominant source of experiential knowledge, it immediately raises a critical question. We gather knowledge by seeing, touching, hearing.

**II. Introspection:** Introspection is like a sixth sense that looks into the most intimate parts of our minds, which allows us to inspect how we are feeling and how our thoughts are operating. If I go to a doctor complaining of an aching back, she'll ask me to describe my pain. Through introspection I then might report, "Well, it's a sharp pain that starts right here and stops right here." The doctor herself cannot directly experience what I do and must rely on my introspective report.

**III. Memory:** a memory is like a recording device that captures events that one can experience more or less in the order that they occurred.

**IV. Testimony:** Testimonies from written sources are usually more reliable than oral sources, but much depends on the integrity of the author, publisher, and the methods of verification.

### Scientific Method

### Examples of Scientific Method in Physics

**Observation:** You notice that water boils at different temperatures at different altitudes.

**Question:** Does altitude affect the boiling point of water?

**Hypothesis:** The higher the altitude, the lower the boiling point of water.

**Experiment:** You decide to heat water at different altitudes and record the boiling temperature.

**Analysis:**

| Altitude (m) | Boiling point of water (°C) |
|:-------------|:----------------------------|
| 0            | 100                         |
| 150          | 99.5                        |
| 305          | 99                          |
| 610          | 98                          |
| 1524         | 95                          |

**Conclusion:** The data supports the hypothesis that higher altitudes result in lower boiling points.

## 1.4 The Mission of Physics and Career Awareness

**At the end of this section you will be able to:**

### Typical Careers in Physics

**Figure 1: Career opportunities in physics and related fields**

### Physics Careers in Space and Astronomy
Physicists in this field explore celestial phenomena, planetary science, and cosmology. They work with space agencies, observatories, and research institutions to study the universe.

### Physics Careers in Healthcare
Medical physicists apply physics principles to develop and improve medical imaging technologies, radiation therapy, and diagnostic equipment. They ensure patient safety and treatment effectiveness.

### Physics Careers in Engineering
Physics graduates contribute to various engineering disciplines including electrical, mechanical, civil, and aerospace engineering. They solve complex problems and innovate new technologies.

### Physics Careers in Energy
Physicists work on developing sustainable energy solutions, improving power generation efficiency, and researching alternative energy sources like solar, wind, and nuclear power.

### Physics Careers in Technology
Careers in this area include semiconductor design, quantum computing, photonics, and materials science. Physicists drive innovation in computing and communication technologies.

### Geophysics and Meteorology Careers
Geophysicists study Earth processes and structures, while meteorologists apply physics to understand and predict weather patterns and climate change.

### Research Scientist Careers
Research scientists conduct experiments, analyze data, and publish findings that advance our understanding of physical phenomena across many specialized fields.

### Major Recent Discoveries in Physics

#### Discovery of Exoplanets
Astronomers have discovered thousands of planets orbiting stars beyond our solar system. In 2019, Michel Mayor and Didier Queloz won the Nobel Prize for discovering 51 Pegasi b, the first exoplanet orbiting a solar-type star.

**Figure 9: Artist's impression of exoplanet orbiting two stars**  
*The image shows a conceptual visualization of a circumbinary planet system.*

#### Black Hole Discoveries
Roger Penrose, Reinhard Genzel, and Andrea M. Ghez received the 2020 Nobel Prize for their work on black hole formation and discovering the supermassive black hole at the center of our galaxy.

#### Quantum Cryptography
Advances in quantum information science have enabled ultra-secure communication systems based on quantum mechanics principles.

#### High Energy Physics (Particle Accelerators)
The Large Hadron Collider and other accelerators continue probing fundamental particles, including discoveries related to the Higgs boson and exotic matter.

#### Gravitational Wave Detection
LIGO and Virgo observatories have detected gravitational waves from merging black holes and neutron stars, confirming Einstein's predictions and opening a new window for astronomy.

#### Global Warming Research

Physics plays a crucial role in climate modeling and understanding anthropogenic impacts on Earth's systems.

#### James Webb Space Telescope (JWST)
Launched in December 2021, JWST operates at extremely cold temperatures (-223°C) and provides unprecedented infrared observations of the early universe, exoplanet atmospheres, and stellar formation.

### Future of Physics
Emerging fields include quantum computing, fusion energy research, nanotechnology applications, and deeper investigations into dark matter and dark energy.

## UNIT SUMMARY

Physics improves our quality of life by providing the basic understanding necessary for developing new instrumentation and techniques for medical applications, such as computer tomography, magnetic resonance imaging, positron emission tomography, ultrasonic imaging, and laser surgery.

Transportation vehicles such as automobiles, airplanes and space shuttles could not be constructed without the help of physics experts.

Physics is also useful for military purposes, informing the design of weapons. Many of the scientists responsible for inventing the atomic bomb were physicists, and today physicists are involved in the creation of nuclear weapons.

Physics communities are an organized group of persons associated together for scientific purposes. This organization is established at the level of national, continent as well as worldwide based on their aims.

The scientific method is an ordered series of steps to acquire knowledge based on experimental evidence.

The mission of physics is to advance science, engineering, and innovation throughout the world for the benefit of all and serving Society.

Physics have a lot of job opportunities in the fields of Astronomy, healthcare, engineering, energy, technology, meteorology, etc.

New discoveries of Exoplanets, Black hole, Quantum cryptography, High energy physics, Gravitational wave, Global warming, James Webb Space Telescope, etc have been seen in recent years.
