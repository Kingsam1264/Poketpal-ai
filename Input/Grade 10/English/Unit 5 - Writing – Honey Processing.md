# Unit 5: Writing – Honey Processing

## Unit Objectives

Upon completion of this lesson, you will be able to:

- Listen to expository texts from spoken sources and identify the most essential ideas  
- Describe different processes that are important in making or manufacturing something  
- Read books, factual articles, magazines, etc., analyze their themes or other essential features, and personalize the information into your life  
- Use a word-attack skill, increase your vocabulary store, and use newly learned words in your communications  
- List out words coined through clipping and blending and use them in different sentences  
- Apply phrasal verbs attached to the verbs "get" and "give" in both spoken and written English  
- Practice using the present simple tense in both active and passive voice forms  
- Familiarize yourself with subject-verb agreement rules and use them in constructing correct sentences  
- Differentiate the use of "make" and "do" and use them correctly in your own sentences

### Reading Passage: The Importance of Honey

Albert Einstein once said, *"The day bees vanish from earth... man will go not long after."* Honey has nutritional, cosmetic, therapeutic, and industrial value. It is a natural sweetener composed mainly of carbohydrates (95–97% of dry weight), including fructose and glucose. It never spoils and can be stored at room temperature.

Honey is used for:

- Wound healing  
- Burn and eye inflammation treatment  
- Digestive aid and laxative  
- Heart health support  
- Cold and respiratory relief  
- Kidney function improvement  
- Memory and mental clarity enhancement  
- Dental health (antibacterial properties)

## 5.5 Grammar

### 5.5.1 Simple Present Active and Passive

**Pattern:**  
Passive = Object + am/is/are + past participle

**Examples:**  
- Active: The president makes a speech.  
- Passive: A speech is made by the president.

---

### Activity

Convert to passive:

1. People speak English everywhere.  
→ English is spoken everywhere.

2. I have not seen such a lion.  
→ Such a lion has not been seen by me.

3. You ought to take photographs.  
→ Photographs ought to be taken.

4. Our father does not let us watch films.  
→ We are not let to watch films by our father.

5. We had better do homework.  
→ Homework had better be done.

6. Students need to carry information.  
→ Information needs to be carried by students.

7. Neither child draws this picture.  
→ This picture is drawn by neither child.

8. Experiments prove discoveries.  
→ Discoveries are proven by experiments.

9. The smoke fills the room.  
→ The room is filled with smoke.

10. Students have been reading notes.  
→ Notes have been being read by students.

---

### 5.5.2 Subject-Verb Agreement

**Rules:**

- Singular subject → singular verb  
- Plural subject → plural verb  
- "One of", "either of", "neither of" → singular verb  
- Compound subjects with "together with", "along with" → verb agrees with first subject  
- "Either...or" / "Neither...nor" → verb agrees with closer subject

---

## 5.6 Writing

### 5.6.1 Writing a Topic Sentence

A topic sentence carries the main idea of a paragraph. It can be placed at the beginning, middle, or end.

**Example:**  
**Haile Gebresillassie is one of Ethiopia's most famous long-distance runners.**
