### 7.1.1 The Idea of Climate Change

Climate change refers to the long-term changes in global temperatures and other characteristics of the atmosphere. Global temperature is rising, weather patterns are changing, polar ice is melting, and sea level is rising. Climate has changed throughout Earth's long history, but this time it's different. Climate change is particularly apparent from the mid to late 20th century onwards and is attributed largely to the increased levels of atmospheric carbon dioxide produced by the use of fossil fuels.

### 7.1.2 Causes and Effects of Climate Change

There are different factors responsible for climate change. They include both human and natural factors.

#### I. Human Factors Responsible for Climate Change

**A. Urbanization:**  
Cities are warmer and more polluted than the countryside. For example, in rural areas, a large part of the incoming solar energy evaporates water from vegetation and soil. Whereas, in cities, much of the sun's heat is absorbed by buildings and asphalted surfaces.

**B. Global Warming:**  
Global warming is a gradual increase in the earth's temperature generally due to the greenhouse effect caused by increased levels of carbon dioxide, CFCs, and other pollutants. Human activities, primarily fossil fuel burning, increase heat-trapping greenhouse gas levels in Earth's atmosphere. Carbon dioxide and water vapor in the atmosphere trap heat energy from the sun. This process is called the **Greenhouse Effect**.

**Focus: Greenhouse Gases are:**

- **Chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs):** refrigerants, aerosols, foams  
- **Methane (CH₄):** animal waste, biomass burning, fossil fuels, landfills, livestock, rice paddies, sewage, wetlands  
- **Nitrous Oxide (N₂O):** biomass burning, deforestation, burning of fossil fuels, microbial activity on fertilizers  
- **Water vapor (H₂O):** evaporation, plant respiration  

The greenhouse effect is a natural process that warms the Earth's surface. The Earth's atmosphere acts like the glass in a greenhouse. The major greenhouse gases are water vapor, carbon dioxide, chlorofluorocarbons, methane, and nitrous oxide. Of these, water vapor and carbon dioxide play a great role in the absorption of heat.

**C. Nuclear War:**  
A nuclear war would raise an enormous amount of thick smoke from massive fires that would burn for days or even weeks. The smoke would move higher into the atmosphere and increase Earth's temperature.

**D. Ozone Layer Depletion:**  
The ozone layer is found in the stratosphere. It absorbs much of the sun's ultraviolet radiation, preventing it from reaching the earth. Gases called CFCs (Chlorofluorocarbons) destroy ozone gas. They are used in refrigerators and aviation. Holes have been discovered in the ozone layer above the Arctic Circle and Antarctica.

**E. Acid Rain:**  
Coal and oil release a mixture of gases as they burn. These gases combine with water vapor, sunlight, and oxygen to form sulphuric acid and nitric acid, which fall back to earth as acid rain. This rain can kill plants and fish, leach nutrients from the soil, damage buildings, and reduce agricultural and forest productivity.

#### II. Natural Factors Responsible for Climate Change

**Startup Questions:**  
What are the naturally induced factors responsible for global climate change?

**A. Oceans:**  
The oceans play a major role in the formation of the world climate system. They are huge storehouses for carbon dioxide and heat energy, influencing the global pattern and speed of climate change.

**B. Clouds:**  
Clouds reflect incoming sunlight and absorb infrared radiation from the earth, having both cooling and warming effects.

### Effects of Climate Change on Human Life and the Physical Environment

Climate change threatens human health and well-being in numerous ways. Health effects include:

- Increased respiratory and cardiovascular disease  
- Injuries and premature deaths related to extreme weather events  
- Infectious diseases  
- Mental health impacts  

**Environmental Effects:**

- **Desertification:** Persistent degradation of dryland ecosystems  
- **Droughts, famines, and starvations:** Damage to crops, animals, and human life  
- **Rise in sea level:** Melting glaciers and expanding oceans affect marine life and contaminate groundwater  
- **Loss of biodiversity:** Extinction of plant and animal species  
- **Disruption of the hydrological cycle:** Alters natural water distribution

## 7.1.3 The Development of Climate Action

**Competency:** At the end of this lesson, you will be able to:  
► Discuss the role of climate actions in confronting climate change.

### Key Terms

- Adaptation measure  
- Green economy  
- Climate action  
- Mitigation measure  

### Startup Question

What are possible ways to mitigate climate change?

Climate action means stepped-up efforts to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and strengthen resilience and adaptive capacity to climate-induced impacts, including climate-related hazards. Global climate change is largely caused by the combustion of fossil fuels and deforestation.

### Mitigation and Adaptation to Climate Change

**Mitigation measures** address the causes of climate change.  
**Adaptation measures** address the impacts.

#### How to Mitigate Climate Change?

- Practice energy efficiency  
- Use renewable energy  
- Implement efficient transport (electric public transport, bicycles)  
- Carbon tax and emission markets  

#### Adaptation to Climate Change

- More secure facilities and infrastructure  
- Landscape restoration (e.g., reforestation)  
- Research and development on catastrophes and temperature behavior  
- Preventive and precautionary measures (evacuation plans, health issues)

## 7.1.4 Case Studies on Issues in Your Locality

**Case Study: Ethiopia's Climate Resilience Green Economy (CRGE) Strategy**

Ethiopia's mitigation policy is based on the CRGE strategy, which aims to keep greenhouse gas emissions low and build climate resilience while achieving middle-income status by 2025. The strategy is based on four pillars:

1. Reducing agricultural emissions  
2. Protecting and expanding forests  
3. Expanding renewable electricity generation  
4. Adopting energy-efficient technologies in transport, industry, and buildings  

The Ethiopian government plans to:

- Increase fuel efficiency standards  
- Promote hybrid and electric vehicles  
- Construct a renewable energy-powered electric rail network  
- Improve public transportation in Addis Ababa  
- Increase the use of biofuels

## 7.1.5 Air Pollution

**Competency:** At the end of this lesson, you will be able to:  
► Describe the effects of air pollution.

### Key Terms

- Air pollution  
- Pollutant  
- Carbon dioxide  
- Pollution  

### Startup Question: Define

**Pollution** is an undesirable change in the physical, chemical, or biological characteristics of air, water, and land that may be harmful to human life and other animals.

**Air Pollution** is the presence of materials in the air in such concentration that are harmful to humans and the environment.

### Sources of Air Pollution

1. **Burning Fossil Fuels:** Releases CO₂, SO₂  
2. **Emissions from Automobiles:** CO, unburnt hydrocarbons, nitrogen oxides  
3. **Industrial Emissions:** CO, SO, NO, hydrocarbons, cotton dust  
4. **Agricultural Activities:** Insecticides and weedicides  
5. **Wars:** Explosives and radioactive gases  
6. **Natural Causes:** Volcanic gases, marsh gas, fungal spores, pollens

## 7.2 Water Shortage / Scarcity

**Competency:** At the end of this lesson, you will be able to:  
► List problems related to water shortage and scarcity.

### Key Terms

- Water scarcity  
- Water conservation  

### Startup Questions

- What is water scarcity?  
- What are the problems related to water shortage and scarcity?

Water shortage/scarcity is a lack of sufficient water or access to safe water supplies. It is caused by natural and human activities. Water is unequally distributed over time and space, and much of it is wasted, polluted, or unsustainably managed.

### Impacts of Water Scarcity

- Affects crops and livestock → food shortages → starvation  
- Sanitation problems → unsafe drinking water → diseases like trachoma, plague, typhus  
- Environmental harm: increased salinity, nutrient pollution, loss of floodplains  

### Water Conservation at Home

- Use water efficiently  
- Change daily habits  
- Use only necessary water

**Competencies:** At the end of this lesson you will be able to:  
► Explain the effects of deforestation;  
► Value individuals and communities' commitment to protect the environment.

### Key Terms

- Deforestation  
- Soil erosion

- Urbanization  

### Startup Questions

- Define deforestation in your own words.  
- Explain the effects of deforestation.  
- Describe the commitment of individuals/communities to protect the environment.

**Deforestation** refers to the indiscriminate cutting of trees. Forests are cleared for agriculture, urbanization, or mining.

### Causes of Deforestation

1. Expansion of agricultural activities  
2. Construction of infrastructures  
3. Rapid urbanization  
4. Demand for fuel wood  

### Effects of Deforestation

1. **Loss of Habitat:** Species lose shelter and temperature regulation  
2. **Increased Greenhouse Gases:** Fewer trees → more CO₂  
3. **Depletion of Water in the Atmosphere:** Disrupts water cycle  
4. **Erosion and Flooding:** Soil loses nutrients and becomes prone to flooding

## Summary
