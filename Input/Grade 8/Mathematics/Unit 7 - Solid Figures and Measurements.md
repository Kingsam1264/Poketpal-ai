### 1. Prisms

**Definition 7.1:** A prism is a solid figure in which two of the faces, called the bases of the prism, are congruent polygons in parallel planes.

Depending on the shape of its base a prism can be triangular prism, rectangular prism, pentagonal prism, hexagonal prism and so on.

**Figure 7.3:** Diagram showing a rectangular prism with labeled vertices (A, B, C, D, E, F, G, H).

Consider the rectangular prism as shown below:
- The vertices of the rectangular prism are A, B, C, D, E, F, G, and H
- A prism has two bases: upper base and lower base. The rectangular region ABCD is the upper base and rectangular region EFGH is the lower base.
- The line segments AB, BC, CD, DA, EF, FG, GH, HE are edges of the bases.
- The line segments AE, BF, CG, and DH (the segments that connect the vertices) are the lateral edges of the prism.
- The rectangles ABFE, BCGF, CDHG, and ADHE (the surfaces between corresponding sides of the bases) are called the lateral faces of the prism.
- An altitude of a prism is a line segment perpendicular to each of the bases with an end point on each base. The height of a prism is the length of an altitude.

**Definition 7.2:** A right prism is a prism in which the lateral sides are all perpendicular to the bases.

#### Note:
- Solid figures can be identified by the shape of their bases, number of bases and the shape of their lateral faces.
- The bases of a prism are parallel.
- The corresponding sides of the bases of a prism are congruent and parallel.
- Every lateral edge of a prism are parallel and congruent.
- Every lateral face of a prism is a parallelogram.
- Every lateral face of a right prism is a rectangle.

#### Example
The bases of a right prism are equilateral triangles.
a) How many lateral edges does this prism have?
b) What is the shape of the lateral face?

**Solution:**
a) Because this is a prism with a triangular base, the prism has three lateral edges.
b) Because it is a right prism, the lateral faces are rectangles.

### 2. Cylinders

**Definition 7.3:** A cylinder is a solid figure with congruent non-polygonal bases that lie in parallel planes. If the bases are circular, the cylinder is a circular cylinder.

- The line passing through the centers of the two circular bases is called the axis of the cylinder.
- The altitude of a cylinder is the perpendicular distance between its bases.
- The radius of the base is also called the radius of the cylinder.
- A cylinder is called a right cylinder if the segment joining the centers of the bases is perpendicular to the bases.

### 1. Pyramids

**Definition 7.4:** A pyramid is a solid figure in which the base is a polygon and the lateral faces are triangles with a common vertex.

From Figure 7.6, we define the following terminologies:
- The polygonal region ABCD is called the base of the pyramid.
- The point V outside of the plane of the base is called the vertex of the pyramid.
- The triangles VAB, VBC, VCD, and VDA are called lateral faces of the pyramid.
- The intersection of two lateral faces is called lateral edges. Thus, VA, VB, VC, and VD are lateral edges of the pyramid.
- The intersection of the base and a lateral face is a base edge. Thus, AB, BC, CD, and DA are the edges of the base of the pyramid.
- The altitude of the pyramid is the perpendicular distance between the base and the vertex.
- A pyramid whose altitude passes through the center of the base is called a right pyramid.

### A. Surface Area of Prisms

There are two types of surface areas: Lateral surface area and Total surface area.

**Definition 7.7:** The lateral surface area, denoted by Aₗ, is the sum of the areas of all lateral faces.

#### Example
Derive a formula for the lateral surface area of a rectangular prism using its net.

**Solution:**
The surface area of a right rectangular prism can be calculated by representing the 3D figure into a 2D net; to make the shapes easier to see. After expanding the 3D shape into 2D shape we will get six rectangles (See Figure 7.12). Pairwise (Front and back, right and left, top and bottom) rectangles have equal size.

Thus:
- Area of front face = area of back face = l × h
- Area of left face = area of right face = w × h
- Area of top face = area of bottom face = l × w

Since the lateral surface area is the sum of the areas of all lateral faces, then:

Aₗ = area of front face + area of back face + area of left face + area of right face
   = lh + lh + wh + wh
   = 2lh + 2wh
   = 2h(l + w)
   = ph

where p = 2(l + w) is the perimeter of the base.

**Definition 7.8:** The total surface area, denoted by Aₜ, is the sum of the areas of all the faces.

Total surface area (Aₜ) = Aₗ + area of top face + area of bottom face
= ph + lw + lw
= ph + 2lw
= Aₗ + 2A_B

where A_B = lw is the area of the base.

#### Example 7.4
Derive a formula for the lateral surface area of a rectangular prism using its 3D shape.

**Solution:**
Refer to the 3D shape of a rectangular prism (Figure 7.12). It has six faces: two bases and four lateral faces.

Lateral Surface Area = sum of the area of the four lateral faces.
Aₗ = area(ABGH) + area(ADEH) + area(DCFE) + area(BCFG)
   = wh + lh + wh + lh
   = 2lh + 2wh
   = 2h(l + w)
   = ph, where p = 2(l + w) is the perimeter of the base.

Total Surface Area = Aₗ + areas of the two bases
= Aₗ + area(ABCD) + area(EFGH)
= Aₗ + 2lw
= Aₗ + 2A_B

**In general:**
- The lateral surface area of a prism is Aₗ = ph, where p is the perimeter of the base.
- The total surface area of a prism is Aₜ = Aₗ + 2A_B, where A_B is the area of the base.

**Figure 7.12:** 3D representation of a rectangular prism showing front, back, left, right, top, and bottom faces.

### B. Surface Area of Cylinders

To derive a formula for the surface area of a circular cylinder, consider the net of the cylinder. It has two congruent and parallel circular bases and a rectangular face of length 2πr and width h as shown in Figure 7.13.

Therefore:
- The lateral surface area (Aₗ) = area of the rectangle = 2πrh
- The total surface area (Aₜ) = Aₗ + area of the bases = 2πrh + 2πr² = 2πr(h + r)

**In general:**
- The lateral surface area of a circular cylinder is Aₗ = 2πrh
- The total surface area of a circular cylinder is Aₜ = 2πr(h + r)

**Figure 7.13:** Net of a cylinder showing the rectangular lateral face with dimensions 2πr × h and two circular bases.

---

#### Example 7.7
Find the total surface area of a right circular cylinder whose radius is 8 cm and height is 12 cm.

**Solution:**
Aₜ = 2πr(h + r) = 2π × 8 × (12 + 8) = 320π cm²

---

#### Example 7.8
Find the height of the right circular cylinder if its lateral surface area is 108π cm² and radius of the cylinder is 6 cm.

**Solution:**
Aₗ = 2πrh
108π = 2π × 6 × h = 12πh
h = 108π / 12π = 9 cm

Therefore, the height of the cylinder is 9 cm.

---

#### Example 7.9
The total surface area of a right circular cylinder of radius 7 cm is 112π cm². Then find the height of the cylinder.

**Solution:**
Aₜ = 2πr(h + r)
112π = 2π × 7 × (h + 7)
112π / 14π = h + 7
8 = h + 7
h = 1 cm

Therefore, the height of the cylinder is 1 cm.

---

#### Example 7.10
The sum of the height and the radius of a right circular cylinder is 9 cm. If the surface area of the cylinder is 54π cm², then find the height of the cylinder.

**Solution:**
Let h be the height and r be the radius of the cylinder.
h + r = 9
Aₜ = 2πr(h + r)
54π = 2πr × 9
54π / 2π = 9r
27 = 9r
r = 3 cm

Since h + r = 9, h = 9 - 3 = 6 cm

Therefore, the height of the cylinder is 6 cm.

## 7.2.2 Volume of Prisms and Cylinders

**Competency:** At the end of this subtopic, students should be able to find the volume of prisms and cylinders.

As area is thought of as the number of square units that can be contained within a two-dimensional boundary, the volume of a solid can be thought of as the number of cubic units that will fit within the boundary of a three-dimensional solid.

**Definition 7.9:** The volume of a solid is the number of cubic units contained in its interior. That is, the amount of space it occupies. Volume is measured in cubic units such as m³, cm³, etc.

### A. Volume of Prism

The volume V of a rectangular prism is equal to the product of its length (l), width (w), and height (h). That is, V = l × w × h. By using this relationship, a special formula can be derived for determining the volume of a cube. That is, V = l × l × l = l³.

**Figure 7.14:** Rectangular prism with dimensions length (l), width (w), and height (h).

The volume V of a right triangular prism equals the product of its base area A_B and its height h. That is, V = A_B × h.

**Figure 7.15:** Triangular prism showing base area and height.

**Definition 7.10:** The volume V of a prism is V = A_B × h, where A_B is the area of the base and h is the height.

#### Example 7.10
Determine the height of a rectangular prism that has base dimensions of 8 cm, 6 cm and a volume of 312 cm³.

**Solution:**
Let l = 8 cm and w = 6 cm.
V = l × w × h
312 cm³ = 8 × 6 × h
h = 312 / 48 = 6.5 cm

Therefore, the height of the prism is 6.5 cm.

---

#### Example 7.11
The bases of a right prism are ΔABC and ΔEFG. Let D be a point on BC and AD is perpendicular to BC.

If AB = 10 cm, AC = 10 cm, BC = 12 cm, AD = 8 cm and EB = 15 cm, what is the volume of the prism?

**Solution:**
The length of the base is BC = 12 cm, and the length of the altitude of the base is AD = 8 cm.

Area of ΔABC = ½ × BC × AD = ½ × 12 × 8 = 48 cm²

Since the prism is a right prism, the height of the prism is BE = 15 cm.

Volume of the prism = A_B × h = 48 × 15 = 720 cm³

**Figure 7.16:** Right triangular prism with base ΔABC and height BE.

---

#### Example 7.12
The base of a prism is an equilateral triangle each of whose sides measure 4 cm. If the altitude of the prism measures 5 cm, then find the volume of the prism.

**Solution:**
Since the base is an equilateral triangle, first determine the height of the base (y):
y² + 2² = 4²
y² = 16 - 4 = 12
y = √12 cm

Area of base = ½ × base × height = ½ × 4 × √12 = 2√12 cm²

Then, V = A_B × h = 2√12 × 5 = 10√12 cm³

**Figure 7.17:** Equilateral triangular base with side length 4 cm and height y.

### A. Volume of Cylinder

#### Group Work 7.4: Discuss with your groups

Take two sheets of paper that measure 12 cm by 28 cm and form two cylinders, one with the height as 12 cm and one with the height as 28 cm. Do the cylinders have the same volume? Explain.

**Figure 7.18:** Two cylinders formed from the same sheet of paper but with different orientations.

The formula for the volume of the cylinder can be derived easily because a cylinder can be thought of as a prism with a circular base. The volume of a prism is V = A_B × h. Since the base of a cylinder is a circle, then the base area A_B is the area of a circle, A = πr². By substitution, the volume of a cylinder can be found by using the formula V = πr²h.

**Definition 7.11:** The volume V of a cylinder is V = πr²h.

#### Example 7.13
A right circular cylinder of height 12 cm has a volume of 972π cm³. Find the radius of the base of the cylinder.

**Solution:**
V = πr²h
972π = π × r² × 12
r² = 972π / 12π = 81
r = 9 cm

Therefore, the radius of the base is 9 cm.

---

#### Example 7.14
Consider a circular cylinder. If a new cylinder is formed by doubling the radius and halving the height, then what is the volume of the new cylinder?

**Solution:**
Let V₁ be the volume of the first cylinder. Let r₁ and h₁ be the respective radius and height of the first cylinder.

Since r₂ = 2r₁ and h₂ = ½h₁, then:
V₂ = πr₂²h₂ = π(2r₁)² × ½h₁ = 4 × ½ × πr₁²h₁ = 2πr₁²h₁ = 2V₁

Therefore, the volume of the new cylinder becomes twice that of the original cylinder.

### Example 7.15
A truck that delivers gasoline has a circular cylindrical storage space. The diameter of the bases of the cylinder is 34 cm and the height of the cylinder is 46 cm. How many gallons of gasoline does the truck hold? (Use 1 cm³ = 0.000264 gal)

**Solution:**
V = πr²h = π × 17² × 46 = 13,294π cm³

Since 1 cm³ = 0.000264 gal:
V = 13,294π × 0.000264 gal = 3.51π gal

Therefore, the truck holds 3.51π gallons of gasoline.

---

### Example 7.16
The walls, floor, and ceiling of a room form a rectangular solid. The total surface area of the room is 992 m². The dimensions of the floor are 12 m by 20 m.

a) What is the lateral surface area of the room?
b) What is the height of the room?

**Solution:**
a) Since the solid is a rectangular prism, then:
Aₜ = Aₗ + 2A_B
992 = Aₗ + 2(20 × 12)
Aₗ = 992 - 480 = 512 m²

Therefore, the area of the walls of the room is 512 m².

b) Since the solid is a rectangular prism, then:
Aₗ = 2(l + w)h
512 = 2(20 + 12)h
512 = 64h
h = 8 m

Therefore, the height of the room is 8 m.

---

### Example 7.17
A fish tank in the form of a rectangular solid is to accommodate 6 fish, and each fish needs at least 7500 cm³ of space. The dimensions of the base are to be 30 cm by 60 cm. What is the minimum height that the tank needs to be?

**Solution:**
The minimum volume occupied by 6 fish is 6 × 7500 = 45,000 cm³

Since the tank is in the form of a rectangular solid:
V = l × w × h
30 × 60 × h ≥ 45,000
1800h ≥ 45,000
h ≥ 25 cm

Therefore, the minimum height is 25 cm.

---

### Example 7.18
A rectangular gift has dimensions of 20 cm by 35 cm by 4 cm. You have wrapping paper with dimensions of 43 cm by 50 cm. Do you have enough wrapping paper to wrap the gift?

**Solution:**
Total Surface Area of the gift = 2(20×35) + 2(20×4) + 2(35×4) = 1840 cm²
Area of wrapping paper = 43 × 50 = 2150 cm²

Therefore, there is enough wrapping paper to wrap the gift.

### Example 7.19
Saron wants to build a right circular cylinder out of cardboard with bases that have a radius of 6 cm and a height of 14 cm.

a) How many square centimeters of cardboard are needed for the cylinder?
b) What will be the volume of the cylinder?

**Solution:**
a) The amount of materials needed implies the total surface area of the cylinder:
Aₜ = 2πrh + 2πr² = 2π × 6 × 14 + 2π × 6² = 168π + 72π = 240π cm²

Therefore, she needs 240π cm² of cardboard to build the cylinder.

b) V = πr²h = π × 6² × 14 = 504π cm³

Therefore, the volume of the constructed cylinder is 504π cm³.

### Example 7.20
A Cereal Company wants to change the shape of its cereal box to attract shoppers. The original cereal box has dimensions of 8 cm by 3 cm by 11 cm. The new box would have dimensions of 10 cm by 10 cm by 3 cm.

a) Which box holds more cereal?
b) Which box requires more material to make?

**Solution:**
a) Volume of original box = 8 × 3 × 11 = 264 cm³
   Volume of new box = 10 × 10 × 3 = 300 cm³

Therefore, the new box holds more cereal than the original box.

b) Surface Area of original box = 2(8×3) + 2(8×11) + 2(3×11) = 290 cm²
   Surface Area of new box = 2(10×10) + 2(10×3) + 2(10×3) = 320 cm²

Therefore, the new box requires more material than the original box.

1. A cylinder whose segments are perpendicular to the bases is called a right cylinder.
2. A cylinder whose bases are enclosed by circles is called a circular cylinder.
3. If the base of the cylinder is a polygonal region, then the solid is called a prism.
4. A prism whose bases are enclosed by triangles is a triangular prism.
5. A prism whose bases are enclosed by rectangles is a rectangular prism.
6. The perpendicular distance between the bases of a prism is the altitude of the prism.
7. The sum of the areas of lateral faces is called lateral surface area and the sum of the lateral surface area and area of the bases is the total surface area.
8. If the lateral edges of the prism are perpendicular to the bases, then the prism is a right prism.
9. The lateral edges of a prism are congruent and parallel.
10. A cube is a three-dimensional solid object bounded by six square faces.
11. A net is a pattern of shapes on a piece of paper that are arranged so that the net can be folded to make a hollow surface.
12. Measuring the surface constituting the solid is called the surface area of the solid figure.
13. The lateral surface area of a right prism is equal to the product of the height and the perimeter of the base. That is, Aₗ = ph, where p is base perimeter and h is the height of the prism.
14. The lateral surface area of a circular cylinder is Aₗ = 2πrh and the total surface area of a circular cylinder is Aₜ = 2πr(h + r).
15. The volume of a solid is the number of cubic units contained in its interior. That is, the amount of space it occupies. Volume is measured in cubic units such as m³, cm³, etc.
16. The volume V of a prism is V = A_B × h, where A_B is the area of the base and h is the height.
17. A cylinder has two bases that are congruent circles lying in parallel planes. Like a volume of a prism, a solid that also has two congruent bases, the volume of a cylinder is given by the formula V = A_B × h. In this formula, A_B represents the area of one of the two identical circles which is πr².
18. A pyramid is a solid in which the base is a polygon and the lateral faces are triangles with a common vertex.
19. A circular cone is a solid figure formed by joining all points of a circle to a point not on the plane of the circle.

## REVIEW EXERCISE FOR UNIT 7

1. Write true if the statement is correct and false if it is not:
   a) A cube has 6 faces, 12 edges and 8 vertices.
   b) A cone has one circular face, one vertex and has no edges.
   c) A prism always has 2 parallel faces.
   d) The net of a rectangular prism is made from 4 rectangles.
   e) A triangle is a possible base of a prism.
   f) A pyramid can have a circular base.

2. How many edges has:
   a) a square pyramid?
   b) a cylinder?
   c) a triangular prism?
   d) a cube?

3. Given that the surface area of a rectangular solid is the sum of the area of its six faces. Then:
   a) What is the expected type of solid?
   b) Which type of area is referred?

4. A right prism has bases that are squares. The area of one base is 16 cm². The lateral surface area of the prism is 144 cm². What is the length of the altitude of the prism?

5. The bases of a prism are right triangles whose edges measure 9 cm, 40 cm and 41 cm. The lateral sides of the prism are perpendicular to the bases. The height of the prism is 20 cm.
   a) What is the shape of the lateral sides of the prism?
   b) What are the dimensions of each lateral side of the prism?
   c) What is the total surface area of the prism?

6. If the total surface area of a right circular cylinder is 884π cm² and its radius is 2 cm, then what is the length of the altitude?

7. The sum of the height and radius of a right circular cylinder is 9 cm. If the total surface area is 81π cm², then what is the radius of the cylinder?

8. The length, width and height of a rectangular solid are in the ratio of 3:2:1. If the volume of the box is 384 cm³, what is the total surface area of the box?

9. The dimensions of a box are 16 cm by 11 cm by 9 cm. A small cubical box measures 2 cm on each side. How many of these small boxes fit into the bigger box?

10. The volume of a right circular cylinder is 252 cm³ and the radius of the base is 4 cm. Find the height of the cylinder.

11. The areas of the bases of a cylinder are each 124 cm² and the volume of the cylinder is 116π cm³. Find the height of the cylinder.
