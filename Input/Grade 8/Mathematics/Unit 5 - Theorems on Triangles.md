# Unit 5: Theorems on Triangles 

## Introduction

In geometry, one of the most used shapes is a triangle. A triangle has three sides and three angles. These sides and angles are the elements of the triangle. All polygons have two types of angles: interior angles and exterior angles. As the triangle is the smallest polygon, it has three interior angles and six exterior angles. There are various kinds of triangles with different angles and sides, but all of them follow the triangle sum properties. The two most important properties are the angle sum property of a triangle and the exterior angle property of a triangle. In this unit, you will learn some basic theorems about triangles.

### Theorem 5.1: Angle Sum Theorem  
**The sum of the degree measures of the interior angles of a triangle is equal to 180°.**

#### Proof:
Given: Triangle ABC with AD ∥ BC  
Prove: ∠1 + ∠2 + ∠3 = 180°

| Statements | Reasons |
|------------|---------|
| 1. Triangle ABC with AD ∥ BC | Given |
| 2. ∠4 = ∠1, ∠5 = ∠2 | Alternate interior angles |
| 3. ∠4 + ∠BAD = 180° | Straight angle |
| 4. ∠3 + ∠5 = ∠BAD | Angle addition |
| 5. ∠4 + ∠3 + ∠5 = 180° | Substitution |
| 6. ∠1 + ∠2 + ∠3 = 180° | Substitution |

### Theorem 5.2: Exterior Angle Theorem  
**The measure of an exterior angle of a triangle is equal to the sum of the measures of the two remote interior angles.**

#### Proof:
Let triangle ABC have interior angles *x, y, z* and exterior angle *d*.  
We want to show: *x + y = d*

| Statements | Reasons |
|------------|---------|
| 1. z + d = 180° | Straight angle |
| 2. x + y + z = 180° | Angle Sum Theorem |
| 3. x + y + z = z + d | Substitution |
| 4. x + y = d | Subtract z from both sides |

---

### Example 5.3:
Interior angles: 25° and (x + 15)°  
Exterior angle: (3x − 10)°  
**Solution:**  
3x − 10 = 25 + x + 15  
2x = 50  
x = 25°

## 5.3 Theorems on the Right Angled Triangle

### 5.3.1 Euclid's Theorem and Its Converse

#### Competencies:
- Describe right triangle, altitude, hypotenuse.
- Apply Euclid's theorem and its converse.

---

### Theorem 5.3: Euclid's Theorem  
In a right triangle with an altitude to the hypotenuse:
- (Leg₁)² = Hypotenuse × Adjacent segment
- (Leg₂)² = Hypotenuse × Other segment

Symbolically:
- a² = c × c₂
- b² = c × c₁

---

### Example 5.5:
**Figure 5.20**  
Right triangle ABC with altitude CD, AD = 4 cm, DB = 12 cm  
Find AC and BC.

**Solution:**  
AC² = AB × AD = 16 × 4 = 64 → AC = 8 cm  
BC² = AB × BD = 16 × 12 = 192 → BC = 8√3 cm

---

### Example 5.6:
Isosceles triangle with vertex angle 90°, base = 18 cm. Find leg length.

**Solution:**  
Altitude divides base into 9 cm each.  
AB² = 9 × 18 = 162 → AB = 9√2 cm

---

### Theorem 5.4: Converse of Euclid's Theorem  
If the square of each shorter side equals the product of the longest side and the adjacent segment, then the triangle is right-angled.

---

### Example 5.7:
Given AD = 4 cm, BD = 8 cm, AC = 4√3 cm, BC = 4√6 cm  
Check if triangle ABC is right-angled.

**Solution:**  
AC² = 48 = 12 × 4 → b² = c × c₂  
BC² = 96 = 12 × 8 → a² = c × c₁  
**Conclusion:** Triangle is right-angled.

### Theorem 5.5: Pythagoras' Theorem  
In a right triangle:  
**a² + b² = c²**  
where *c* is the hypotenuse.
