### Introduction

In mathematics, the word "similar" has a very specific meaning. Two objects are similar if they have the same shape but are not necessarily the same size. For instance, an overhead projector forms an image on the screen which has the same shape as the image on the transparency but with the size altered. Two figures that have the same shape but not necessarily the same size are called similar figures.

### 4.1.1 Definition and Illustration of Similar Figures

Similar geometric figures are figures which have the same shape but not necessarily the same size. The symbol "~" (read as "is similar to") is used for the term similar.

#### Example 4.1:
A tree and its shadow are similar.

#### Example 4.2:
The two photographs of the same person, one at age 5 years and another at age 50 years, are NOT similar because they don't have the same shape.

#### Similar Polygons

**Definition 4.1:** Two polygons are said to be similar if there is a one-to-one correspondence between their vertices such that:
1. All pairs of corresponding angles are congruent
2. The ratios of the lengths of all pairs of corresponding sides are equal

**Figure 4.2** shows quadrilateral ABCD similar to EFGH (~), where:
- ∠A ≅ ∠E, ∠B ≅ ∠F, ∠C ≅ ∠G, ∠D ≅ ∠H
- AB/EF = BC/FG = CD/GH = AD/EH

#### Example 4.3:
The following pairs of figures are always similar:
a) Any two squares
b) Any two equilateral triangles

#### Example 4.4:
Consider polygons shown: rectangle, square, and rhombus. Which are similar?

**Solution:**
- The square and rectangle are NOT similar because corresponding sides are not proportional
- The square and rhombus are NOT similar because corresponding angles are not congruent
- The rectangle and rhombus are NOT similar because neither corresponding angles nor sides satisfy similarity conditions

**Definition 4.2:** The ratio of two corresponding sides of similar polygons is called the scale factor or constant of proportionality (k).

#### Example 4.5:
The sides of a quadrilateral are 2cm, 5cm, 6cm, and 8cm. Find the sides of a similar quadrilateral whose shortest side is 3cm.

**Solution:**
Let the corresponding sides be 3, x, y, and z. Since corresponding sides are proportional:
- 2/3 = 5/x ⇒ x = 15/2 = 7.5cm
- 2/3 = 6/y ⇒ y = 9cm
- 2/3 = 8/z ⇒ z = 12cm

Therefore, the sides are 3cm, 7.5cm, 9cm, and 12cm.

#### Example 4.6:
Decide whether the polygons are similar. If so, find the scale factor of Figure A to Figure B.

**Figure 4.3** shows two figures with side lengths:
- Figure A: 18, 27, 36
- Figure B: 8, 12, 16

**Solution:**
Corresponding sides are proportional (18/8 = 27/12 = 36/16 = 9/4) and corresponding angles are congruent. The polygons are similar with scale factor 9/4.

### 4.1.2 Similar Triangles

**Definition 4.3:** ∆ABC is similar to ∆DEF (∆ABC ~ ∆DEF) if their:
1. Corresponding sides are proportional: AB/DE = BC/EF = AC/DF = k
2. Corresponding angles are congruent: ∠A ≅ ∠D, ∠B ≅ ∠E, ∠C ≅ ∠F

**Figure 4.4** illustrates similar triangles with corresponding vertices.

#### Example 4.7:
Show that ∆LMN ~ ∆PQR.

**Figure 4.5** shows:
- ∆LMN with angles 105°, 29°, and unknown angle, sides 10cm, 20cm, 15cm
- ∆PQR with angles 105°, 29°, 46°, sides 4cm, 8cm, 6cm

**Solution:**
First find missing angle in ∆LMN: 180° - (105° + 29°) = 46°
Corresponding angles are congruent (105°↔105°, 29°↔29°, 46°↔46°)
Corresponding sides ratio: 10/4 = 20/8 = 15/6 = 2.5
Therefore, ∆LMN ~ ∆PQR with proportionality 2.5.

#### Example 4.8:
Suppose ∆ABC ~ ∆DEF.

**Part a)** Find x and y from figure with corresponding sides:
- (x-2)/6 = 15/10 ⇒ x = 11
- 15/10 = (y+3)/8 ⇒ y = 12

**Part b)** Find a and b from angle measures:
- (a-23)° = 40° ⇒ a = 63
- 80° = (2b-10)° ⇒ b = 35

### 4.1.3 Tests for Similarity of Triangles [AA, SSS, and SAS]

#### Activity 4.2
Melaku said that if an acute angle of one right triangle is congruent to an acute angle of another right triangle, the triangles are similar. Do you agree? Explain.

#### Theorem 4.1 [AA Similarity Theorem]
If two angles of one triangle are congruent to the corresponding two angles of another triangle, then the triangles are similar.

**Example 4.9:**
Suppose DE ∥ BC. Show that ∆ADE ~ ∆ABC.

**Proof:**
1. ∠A ≅ ∠A (Common angle)
2. ∠B ≅ ∠D (Corresponding angles)
3. ∆ADE ~ ∆ABC (AA similarity theorem)

**Conclusion:** A line through two sides of a triangle parallel to the third side cuts off a triangle similar to the given triangle.

#### Example 4.10:
Mustefa is 170cm tall, standing 350cm from a lamp post. His shadow is 80cm long. How high is the lamp post?

**Figure 4.1** shows the scenario with two right triangles.

**Solution:**
Consider ∆ABC (lamp post) and ∆EDC (Mustefa):
- ∠B ≅ ∠D = 90°
- ∠C ≅ ∠C (common)
By AA similarity, ∆ABC ~ ∆EDC.

AB/DE = BC/DC = AC/EC
h/170 = (350+80)/80
h = 913.75cm

The lamp post is 913.75cm tall.

#### Example 4.11:
Find x and y from figure with parallel lines.

**Figure 4.11** shows AB ∥ CD with intersecting lines forming triangles.

**Solution:**
Since AB ∥ CD:
- ∠B ≅ ∠C (Alternate interior angles)
- ∠O ≅ ∠O (Vertically opposite angles)
By AA similarity, ∆ABO ~ ∆DCO.

Thus:
15/21 = y/12 ⇒ y = 60/7
15/21 = 7/x ⇒ x = 49/5

#### Theorem 4.2 [SAS Similarity Theorem]
If an angle of one triangle is congruent to an angle of another triangle and the sides including those angles are proportional, then the triangles are similar.

**Example 4.12:**
Is ∆ABC ~ ∆DEF? Explain.

**Figure 4.13** shows:
- AB = 16, DE = 8
- BC = 12, EF = 6
- ∠B ≅ ∠E = 90°

**Solution:**
AB/DE = 16/8 = 2
BC/EF = 12/6 = 2
∠B ≅ ∠E = 90° (included angles)
Therefore, by SAS similarity, ∆ABC ~ ∆DEF.

#### Example 4.13:
**Figure 4.14** shows triangles with sides and angles.

**Part i)** Show ∆ABC ~ ∆EFD:
- AC/ED = 15/10 = 3/2
- BC/FD = 12/8 = 3/2
- ∠B ≅ ∠F = 40°
Therefore, by SAS similarity, ∆ABC ~ ∆EFD.

**Part ii)** Find x:
(2x+2)/(x+3) = 3/2 ⇒ x = 5

#### Theorem 4.3 [SSS Similarity Theorem]
If the three sides of one triangle are in proportion to the three sides of another triangle, then the triangles are similar.

**Example 4.14:**
Based on the figure, show ∆ABC ~ ∆DEF.

**Figure 4.16** shows:
- AB = 8, DE = 4
- BC = 10, EF = 5
- AC = 16, DF = 8

**Solution:**
AB/DE = 8/4 = 2
BC/EF = 10/5 = 2
AC/DF = 16/8 = 2
Therefore, ∆ABC ~ ∆DEF by SSS Similarity Theorem.

#### Example 4.15:
Given ∆EFD with sides EF = 15cm, FD = 8cm, ED = 12cm. If ∆EFD ~ ∆LMN and the smallest side of ∆LMN is 6cm, find the other sides.

**Solution:**
Smallest side FD = 8cm corresponds to MN = 6cm.
Scale factor = 8/6 = 4/3.

8/6 = 15/LM ⇒ LM = 11.25cm (11¼ cm)
8/6 = 12/LN ⇒ LN = 9cm

Therefore, sides are 6cm, 11¼ cm, and 9cm.

#### Example 4.16:
A 1.80m tall man measures a building's shadow as 28m, while his own shadow is 4.2m. Find the building's height.

**Solution:**
h/28 = 1.8/4.2
h = (1.8 × 28)/4.2 = 12m

The building is 12m tall.

## 4.2 Perimeter and Area of Similar Triangles

### Competency:
At the end of this sub-topic, students should:
- Explain the relation between perimeters of two similar triangles
- Explain the relation between areas of two similar triangles

### Activity 4.3
**Figure 4.19** shows ∆RPQ ~ ∆DEF:
a) Find perimeter P₁ of ∆RPQ and P₂ of ∆DEF
b) Find P₁/P₂ (perimeter ratio)
c) Compare P₁/P₂ and S₁/S₂ (scale factor)
d) Find area A₁ of ∆RPQ and A₂ of ∆DEF
e) Find A₁/A₂ (area ratio)
f) Compare A₁/A₂ and (S₁/S₂)²
g) What do you observe?

#### Example 4.17:
Let ∆ABC ~ ∆PQR with altitudes CD and RE. Determine relationships between:
a) Altitudes
b) Perimeters
c) Areas

**Figure 4.20** illustrates the similar triangles with altitudes.

**Solution:**
a) Let k be scale factor: AB/PQ = BC/QR = AC/PR = k
Since CD and RE are altitudes, ∠D ≅ ∠E (right angles) and ∠B ≅ ∠Q.
Thus ∆CDB ~ ∆REQ by AA similarity, giving CD/RE = CB/RQ = k.

b) p(∆ABC) = AB + BC + AC = k(PQ + QR + PR) = k·p(∆PQR)

c) a(∆ABC) = ½·AB·CD = ½·k·PQ·k·RE = k²·½·PQ·RE = k²·a(∆PQR)

#### Theorem 4.4:
If ratio of corresponding sides is k, then perimeter ratio = k: P₁/P₂ = k

#### Theorem 4.5:
If ratio of corresponding sides is k, then area ratio = k²: A₁/A₂ = k²

#### Example 4.18:
Corresponding sides: 8cm and 6cm. Find ratios:
a) Perimeters: 8/6 = 4/3
b) Areas: (8/6)² = (4/3)² = 16/9

#### Example 4.19:
Areas: 64cm² and 100cm². Side of smaller triangle = 4cm. Find corresponding side of larger.

**Solution:**
64/100 = (4/S₂)² ⇒ S₂ = 5cm

#### Example 4.20:
Side ratio = 2/3. Area of smaller = 36cm². Find area of larger.

**Solution:**
36/A₂ = (2/3)² ⇒ A₂ = 81cm²

#### Example 4.21:
Triangle sides: 16cm, 23cm, 31cm. Similar triangle perimeter = 280cm.
a) Find longest side
b) Find area ratio

**Solution:**
a) P₁ = 70cm, P₂ = 280cm
70/280 = 31/S₂ ⇒ S₂ = 124cm

b) (280/70)² = 16 ⇒ A₂:A₁ = 16:1

## Summary for Unit 4

1. Similar figures have same shape but not necessarily same size.
2. Two polygons are similar if corresponding sides are proportional and corresponding angles congruent.
3. Congruent figures are always similar.
4. Scale factor (k) is ratio of corresponding sides.
5. ∆ABC ~ ∆DEF if corresponding sides are proportional and angles congruent.
6. **AA Theorem:** Two congruent angles ⇒ similar triangles.
7. **SAS Theorem:** One congruent angle with proportional including sides ⇒ similar triangles.
8. **SSS Theorem:** Three proportional sides ⇒ similar triangles.
9. If ∆ABC ~ ∆DEF with scale factor k:
   - p(∆ABC) = k·p(∆DEF)
   - a(∆ABC) = k²·a(∆DEF)
