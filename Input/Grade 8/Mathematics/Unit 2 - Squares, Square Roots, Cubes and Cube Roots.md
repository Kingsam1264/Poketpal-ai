**Note:** There is a difference between *a*² and 2*a*.
- *a*² = *a* × *a*
- 2*a* = *a* + *a*

Consider the following examples to see the difference:
a. 10² = 10 × 10 = 100 whereas 10 × 2 = 20

**Definition 2.2:** A rational number *x* is called a perfect square if and only if *x* = *m*² for some *m* ∈ ℚ.

### Example 2.4:

**Note:**

ii. 0 × 0 = 0. Therefore, 0² = 0  
iii. For any rational numbers *a* and *b*, (*ab*)² = *a*²*b*²  
iv. For any rational numbers *a* and *b*, (*a*/*b*)² = *a*²/*b*² where *b* ≠ 0

**Theorem 2.1: Existence Theorem**  
For any rational number *x*, there is a rational number *y* (*y* ≥ 0) such that *x*² = *y*.

## How to approximate square of a number?

Rough calculation could be carried out for approximating and checking the results in squaring rational numbers. Such approximation depends on rounding off decimal numbers. Rounding is the process to estimate (approximate) a particular number in context. To round a number, look at the next digit in the right place. If the digit is less than 5, round down, and if the digit is 5 or more than 5, round up. Rounding decimals is useful to estimate an answer easily and quickly.

### Example 2.6: Find the approximate value of *x*² in each of the following decimals
a) *x* = 4.3  
b) *x* = 0.026  
c) *x* = 2.45

**Solution:**
a) *x* = 4.3 ≈ 4 [since 3 < 5]  
   *x*² = 4² = 16  
   Therefore, (4.3)² ≈ 16

b) *x* = 0.026 ≈ 0.03  
   *x*² = 0.03² = 0.0009  
   Therefore, (0.026)² ≈ 0.0009

c) *x* = 2.45 ≈ 2.5  
   *x*² = (2.5)² = 6.25  
   Therefore, (2.45)² ≈ 6.25

**Definition 2.3:** For any two rational numbers *a* and *b*; if *a*² = *b*, then *a* is called the square root of *b*.

### Example 2.9:
a) The square root of 4 is 2 because 2² = 4  
b) The square root of 9 is 3 because 3² = 9  
c) The square root of 16 is 4 because 4² = 16  
d) The square root of 25/64 is 5/8 because (5/8)² = 25/64

**Note:**
i. The operation "extracting square root" is the inverse of the operation squaring.
ii. In extracting square roots of rational numbers:
   - First decompose the number into product consisting of two equal factors
   - Take one of the equal factors as the square root of the given number.
iii. The positive square root of a number is called the principal square root. The symbol √ (called the radical sign) is used to indicate the principal square root.
iv. For *b* ≥ 0, the expression √*b* is called the principal square root of *b* or radical *b*, and *b* is called the radicand.
v. The relation between squaring and square root can be expressed as: if *a*² = *b*, then √*b* = *a*.
vi. In power form, √*a* = *a*^(1/2)
vii. Negative rational numbers don't have square roots in the set of rational numbers.
viii. The square root of zero is zero.

### Example 2.10: Find the square root of *x*, if *x* is
a) 16  
b) 121  
c) 0.04  
d) 25/100

**Solution:**
a) *x* = 16 = 4 × 4 = (-4) × (-4)  
   *x* = 4² = (-4)²  
   Thus, the square root of 16 is 4, √16 = 4 [since "√" indicates the positive square root. To indicate the negative square root we use -√, that is -√16 = -4]

b) *x* = 121 = 11 × 11 = (-11) × (-11)  
   *x* = 11² = (-11)²  
   Thus, the square root of 121 is 11, √121 = 11

c) *x* = 0.04 = (0.2)² = (-0.2)²  
   Thus, the square root of 0.04 is 0.2, √0.04 = 0.2

d) *x* = 25/100 = (5/10) × (5/10) = (-5/10) × (-5/10)  
   *x* = (5/10)² = (-5/10)²  
   Thus, the square root of 25/100 is 5/10, √(25/100) = 5/10 = 1/2

### Example 2.11: Find the square root of each of the following numbers by using prime factorization.
a) 64  
b) 100  
c) 400

**Solution:**
a) 64  
   Factorize 64 completely. Then arrange the factors so that 64 is the product of two identical factors:
   64 = 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 2  
   64 = (2 × 2 × 2) × (2 × 2 × 2)  
   64 = 8 × 8  
   64 = 8²  
   So √64 = 8

b) 100  
   Factorize 100 completely. Then arrange the factors so that 100 is the product of two identical factors:
   100 = 2 × 2 × 5 × 5  
   100 = (2 × 5) × (2 × 5)  
   100 = 10 × 10  
   100 = 10²  
   So √100 = 10

c) 400  
   Factorize 400 completely. Then arrange the factors so that 400 is the product of two identical factors:
   400 = 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 5 × 5  
   400 = (2 × 2 × 5) × (2 × 2 × 5)  
   400 = 20 × 20  
   400 = 20²  
   So √400 = 20

**Problem 1:** Alemu and Almaz want to make a square patio. They have concrete to make an area of 400 square meters. How long can a side of their patio be?

**Solution:**
Let *x* be the length of each side of a square patio. Then,  
Area = *x*²  
400 = *x*²  
From this, *x* can be calculated as principal square root: *x* = √400 = 20  
Therefore, the length of each side of a square patio is 20m.

**Problem 2:** The length *X* of the sides of a cube is related to the volume *V* of a cube according to the formula: *X* = ∛*V*. What is the volume of the cube if the side length is 8cm?

**Solution:**
*X* = ∛*V*  
*X*³ = *V*  
8³ = *V*  
Therefore, *V* = 512 cm³

# SUMMARY FOR UNIT 2

1. The process of multiplying a number by itself is called squaring of a number.
2. A rational number *x* is called a perfect square if and only if *x* = *m*² for some *m* ∈ ℚ.
3. The square of rational numbers is also rational numbers.
4. 0 × 0 = 0. Therefore, 0² = 0
5. For any rational numbers *a* and *b*, (*ab*)² = *a*²*b*²
6. For any rational numbers *a* and *b*, (*a*/*b*)² = *a*²/*b*² where *b* ≠ 0
7. For any rational number *x*, there is a rational number *y* (*y* ≥ 0) such that *x*² = *y*
8. For any two rational numbers *a* and *b*, if *a*² = *b*, then *a* is called the square root of *b*, *b* ≥ 0
9. The operation "extracting square root" is the inverse of the operation squaring.
10. In extracting square roots of rational numbers:
    - First decompose the number into product consisting of two equal factors
    - Take one of the equal factors as the square root of the given number.
11. The positive square root of a number is called the principal square root. The symbol "√" (called the radical sign) is used to indicate the principal square root.
12. For *b* ≥ 0, the expression √*b* is called the principal square root of *b* or radical *b*, and *b* is called the radicand.
13. The relation between squaring and square root can be expressed as follows: if *a*² = *b*, then *a* = √*b*.
14. In exponent form, √*a* = *a*^(1/2).
15. Negative rational numbers don't have square roots in the set of rational numbers.
16. The square root of zero is zero.
17. A cube number is a number obtained by multiplying the number by itself three times.
18. A rational number *x* is called a perfect cube if and only if *x* = *n*³ for some *n* ∈ ℚ. That is, a perfect cube is a number that is a product of three identical factors.
19. The cube root of a given number is one of the three identical factors whose product is the given number.
20. 4³ = 64. Then 64 is the cube of 4 and 4 is the cube root of 64. This is written as 4 = ∛64. The symbol ∛ is read as the principal cube root of 64 or simply the cube root of 64.
21. The symbol ∛ is called a radical sign. The expression ∛*a* is called a radical, 3 is called the index, and *a* is called the radicand. When no index is written, the radical sign indicates square root.
22. The relation between cubing and cube root can be expressed as: if *a*³ = *b*, then *a* = ∛*b*.
23. ∛*a* = *a*^(1/3) [exponential form]
24. Each rational number has exactly one cube root.
