**Definition 6.2:** Two triangles are congruent if their corresponding parts (angles and sides) that match one another are equal.

**Note:**
1. Two triangles are congruent if they are copies of each other and when you place one triangle on another, they cover each other completely.
2. If ΔABC is congruent to ΔXYZ, then symbolically written as ΔABC ≅ ΔXYZ

**Correspondence Conditions:**
- X corresponds to A, and ∠X = ∠A
- Y corresponds to B, and ∠Y = ∠B
- Z corresponds to C, and ∠Z = ∠C
- XY corresponds to AB, and XY = AB
- YZ corresponds to BC, and YZ = BC
- XZ corresponds to AC, and XZ = AC

**Example 1:** If ΔABC ≅ ΔFED, then find the six congruent corresponding parts of the triangles.

**Solution:**
- ∠A ≅ ∠F and AB = FE
- ∠B ≅ ∠E and BC = ED
- ∠C ≅ ∠D and AC = FD

### 6.1.3 Tests for Congruence (SAS, SSS, ASA)

In the previous discussion you have seen that to say two triangles are congruent, their six corresponding parts must be congruent. However, in this section you will learn that you do not need all six pieces of information to show triangles are congruent. By using only three parts of a triangle and applying the three tests for congruence (SSS, SAS, ASA), you can show whether two triangles are congruent or not.

#### A. Side-Side-Side (SSS) Congruence Test

**Activity 6.1.3**

**Figures Referenced:**
- Triangle with sides 5cm, 3.4cm, and 5.5cm

1. If only one side of ΔABC is given (say 5.5cm), you cannot draw the exact copy of it because you can draw many triangles using another arbitrary side.
2. If only two sides of ΔABC are given (say 5.5cm and 3.4cm), you cannot draw the exact copy because you can draw many triangles using these two sides and another arbitrary side.
3. If all three sides of ΔABC are given (AB = 5cm, BC = 5.5cm, AC = 3.4cm), you can draw the exact copy.

**Side-Side-Side (SSS) Congruence Test:** If the three sides of one triangle are congruent to the three corresponding sides of another triangle, then the triangles are congruent.

**Example 4:** Examine whether the two triangles are congruent or not. If yes, write the congruence relation in symbolic form.

**Figures Referenced:**
- Triangle ABC with sides: AB = 7.1cm, BC = 5cm, AC = 3.5cm
- Triangle RPQ with sides: RP = 7.1cm, PQ = 5cm, RQ = 3.5cm

**Solution:**
- AB = PR = 7.1cm
- BC = PQ = 5cm
- AC = RQ = 3.5cm

The three sides of one triangle are equal to the three sides of the other triangle, so by SSS congruence test the two triangles are congruent. You can see that A corresponds to R, B corresponds to P, and C corresponds to Q. Hence, ΔABC ≅ ΔRPQ by SSS congruence test.

**Example 5:** Consider the following kite:

**Figures Referenced:**
- Kite ABD with diagonal BD

a. State the three pairs of equal parts in ΔABD and ΔCBD.
b. Is ΔABD ≅ ΔCBD? Why or why not?
c. Does BD bisect ∠ABC? Give reasons.

**Solution:**
a. The three pairs of equal parts in ΔABD and ΔCBD are:
   - AB = CB (given)
   - AD = CD (given)
   - BD = BD (common side)

b. From the above, by SSS, ΔABD ≅ ΔCBD
c. From above, ∠ABD ≅ ∠CBD. So, ∠ABC is bisected by BD.

#### B. Side-Angle-Side (SAS) Congruence Test

**Activity 6.1.4**

**Figures Referenced:**
- Triangle ABC with side BC = 5.5cm, side AC = 3.4cm, and included angle = 65°

1. If only one side of ΔABC is given (say 5.5cm), you cannot draw the exact copy.
2. If one side and one angle are given (say side 5.5cm and angle 65°), you still cannot draw the exact copy because many triangles can be drawn.
3. If two sides and the angle between them are given (BC = 5.5cm, AC = 3.4cm, and the angle between them is 65°), you can draw the exact copy.

**Side-Angle-Side (SAS) Congruence Test:** If two sides and the angle included between them of a triangle are equal to two corresponding sides and the angle included between them of another triangle, then the triangles are congruent.

**Example 6:**

**Figures Referenced:**
- Triangle ABC with AB = AC, AD is a common side, and AD bisects ∠BAC

i. State the three pairs of equal parts in ΔADB and ΔADC.
ii. Is ΔADB ≅ ΔADC? Give reason.
iii. Is ∠B ≅ ∠C? Give reason.
iv. Is BD = DC? Give reason.

**Solution:**
i. The three pairs of equal parts are:
   - AB = AC (Given)
   - AD = AD (Common side)
   - m(∠BAD) = m(∠CAD) (AD bisects ∠BAC)

ii. Yes, ΔADB ≅ ΔADC (by SAS)
iii. Yes, ∠B ≅ ∠C, because they are corresponding parts of congruent triangles.
iv. Yes, BD = DC, because they are corresponding parts of congruent triangles.

#### C. Angle-Side-Angle (ASA) Congruence Test

**Activity 6.1.5**

**Figures Referenced:**
- Triangle with two angles and included side shown

1. If only two angles are given, you cannot draw an exact copy because many triangles can be drawn.
2. If two sides and one angle are given (not between them), you cannot draw an exact copy.
3. If two angles and the side between them are given, you can draw the exact copy.

**Angle-Side-Angle (ASA) Congruence Test:** If two angles and the included side of a triangle are equal to two corresponding angles and the included side of another triangle, then the triangles are congruent.

**Example 7:** ΔABC ≅ ΔQRP by ASA, and BC = RP is given. What additional information is needed to establish ASA congruence test?

**Solution:** For ASA congruence test, we need the two angles between the two sides BC and RP. So the additional information is ∠B ≅ ∠R and ∠C ≅ ∠P.

**Example 8:** Show that ΔAOC ≅ ΔBOD.

**Figures Referenced:**
- Two overlapping triangles AOC and BOD with angles 70°, 30°, and vertical angles

**Solution:**
In ΔAOC: m(∠A) + m(∠C) + m(∠O) = 180°
m(∠A) + 70° + 30° = 180°
m(∠A) = 80°

m(∠AOC) = m(∠DOC) = 30° (vertically opposite angles)

In ΔDOB: m(∠D) + m(∠O) + m(∠B) = 180°
70° + 30° + m(∠B) = 180°
m(∠B) = 80°

Now, AC = DB and the angles that include the sides AC and DB are also congruent (i.e., ∠A ≅ ∠B, and ∠C ≅ ∠D). So by ASA, ΔAOC ≅ ΔBOD.

## 6.2 Applications

After this section, you should be able to apply congruency of plane figures to real-life problems.

In this section you will learn some applications of congruence of triangles.

**Example 1:** Show that the diagonal of a rhombus bisects the angles at the vertex.

**Solution:** Let ABCD be a rhombus. We need to show AC bisects ∠BAD and ∠BCD.

Considering ΔABC and ΔADC:
- AB ≅ AD (All sides of rhombus are congruent)
- BC ≅ DC (All sides of rhombus are congruent)
- AC ≅ AC (Common side for both)

Therefore, ΔABC ≅ ΔADC by SSS congruence test.

∠BAC ≅ ∠DAC (corresponding parts of congruent triangles), hence ∠BAD is bisected by AC.

∠BCA ≅ ∠DCA (corresponding parts of congruent triangles), hence ∠BCD is bisected by AC.

Similarly, we can show BD bisects ∠ABC and ∠ADC.

**Example 2:** In the bridge diagram below, the length of cable AB = 5m, BC = 4m, EF = 4m, AC = 3m. What should be the length of cable DE? (Assume AC ⊥ BC and DF ⊥ FE)

**Figures Referenced:**
- Bridge structure with cables forming triangles ABC and DEF

**Solution:**
In ΔABC and ΔDEF:
- BC = EF = 4m (Given)
- AC = DF = 3m (Common side)
- ∠C ≅ ∠F (Since AC ⊥ BC and DF ⊥ FE)

Therefore, ΔABC ≅ ΔDFE and DE = AB = 5m.

**Example 3:** In the land division diagram:

**Figures Referenced:**
- Land plot with points A, B, C, D, E forming triangles

If BE = CE = 6m, ∠ABC ≅ ∠CED, AE = 4m and AC ⊥ BD, then calculate:
a. CD
b. BD

**Solution:**
a. Consider ΔABC and ΔCDE:
   - BC = CE (Given)
   - ∠ABC ≅ ∠CED (Given)
   - ∠ACB ≅ ∠ECD (Since AC ⊥ BD)
   
   Therefore ΔABC ≅ ΔDEC by ASA. So CD = AC = AE + EC = 4m + 6m = 10m.

b. BD = BC + CD = 6m + 10m = 16m

**Example 4:** In the figure ABCD is a rectangle and F, G, E, and H are the midpoints on sides AB, BC, CD, and AD respectively.

**Figures Referenced:**
- Rectangle ABCD with midpoints F on AB, G on BC, H on AD, and E on CD

Show that:
a. ΔFAH ≅ ΔFBG
b. ΔECG ≅ ΔEDH
c. ΔFAH ≅ ΔEDH
d. HF = FG = EG = EH

**Solution:**
a. Consider ΔFAH and ΔFBG:
   - FA = FB (since F is midpoint of AB)
   - AH = HD (Since AD = BC and H is midpoint of AD, G is midpoint of BC)
   - m(∠A) = m(∠B) = 90° (Angles of rectangle)
   Therefore, ΔFAH ≅ ΔFBG by SAS.

b. Consider ΔECG and ΔEDH:
   - EC = ED (since E is midpoint of CD)
   - CG = HD (Since AD = BC and H is midpoint of AD, G is midpoint of BC)
   - m(∠C) = m(∠D) = 90° (Angles of rectangle)
   Therefore, ΔECG ≅ ΔEDH by SAS.

c. Consider ΔFAH and ΔEDH:
   - FA = ED (since AB = DC, and F is midpoint of AB, E is midpoint of DC)
   - AH = HD (Since H is midpoint of AD)
   - m(∠A) = m(∠D) = 90° (Angles of rectangle)
   Therefore, ΔFAH ≅ ΔEDH by SAS.

d. From a, b, and c you can see that ΔFAH ≅ ΔFBG ≅ ΔECG ≅ ΔEDH. Hence, HF = GF = GE = HE since the corresponding sides of congruent triangles are equal.

## Summary for Unit 6

1. Two figures are congruent if one covers the other completely and exactly.
2. Two line segments are congruent if they have the same length.
3. Two angles are congruent if they have the same measure.
4. Two circles are congruent if they have the same radius.
5. If two figures are congruent, then they have the same shape and size.
6. Two triangles are congruent if their corresponding parts (angles and sides) that match one another are equal.
7. If ΔABC ≅ ΔXYZ, then:
   - X falls on A, and ∠X = ∠A
   - Y falls on B, and ∠Y = ∠B
   - Z falls on C, and ∠Z = ∠C
   - XY = AB, YZ = BC, XZ = AC
8. If the three sides of one triangle are congruent to the three corresponding sides of another triangle, then the triangles are congruent by Side-Side-Side (SSS) congruence rule.
9. If two sides and the angle included between them of a triangle are equal to two corresponding sides and the angle included between them of another triangle, then the triangles are congruent by Side-Angle-Side (SAS) congruence rule.
10. If two angles and the included side of a triangle are equal to two corresponding angles and the included side of another triangle, then the triangles are congruent by Angle-Side-Angle (ASA) congruence rule.
