**Definition 4.1:** The method of comparing two or more quantities of the same kind and in the same units by division is known as a ratio.

The symbol to denote the ratio is `:`. The ratio of one quantity a to another quantity b is usually denoted by a:b. Where a is called antecedent and b is called consequent. The ratio of a to b can be written as any one of the following forms:
- i. a:b → read as a ratio b or a to b
- ii. a/b → read as a over b
- iii. a ÷ b → a divided by b, provided that b ≠ 0

In most cases the ratio a:b is written in simplified form, where a and b are natural numbers. To write the ratio of two quantities, the quantities must have the same units of measurement. Quantities having different units of measurement cannot be compared in the form of ratio. Ratio of quantities has no unit (unitless).

**Example 1:** In a class there are 18 boys and 24 girls.

a. What is the ratio of boys to girls?
b. What is the ratio of girls to boys?
c. What is the ratio of girls to total number of students in the class?
d. What is the ratio of boys to total number of students in the class?

**Solution:**
Total number of students = 18 + 24 = 42

a. Ratio of boys to girls = number of boys / number of girls
   = 18/24 = (18÷6)/(24÷6) = 3/4 or 3:4

b. Ratio of girls to boys = number of girls / number of boys
   = 24/18 = (24÷6)/(18÷6) = 4/3 or 4:3

c. Ratio of girls to total number of students = number of girls / total number of students
   = 24/42 = (24÷6)/(42÷6) = 4/7 or 4:7

d. Ratio of boys to total number of students = number of boys / total number of students
   = 18/42 = (18÷6)/(42÷6) = 3/7 or 3:7

**Example 2:** Write down the ratio of the first number to the second, in the simplest form.

a. 4 to 8
b. 54 to 128
c. 48 to 6
d. 540 to 48

**Solution:**
a. Ratio of 4 to 8 = (4÷4)/(8÷4) = 1/2 or 1:2

b. Ratio of 54 to 128 = (54÷2)/(128÷2) = 27/64 or 27:64

c. Ratio of 48 to 6 = (48÷6)/(6÷6) = 8/1 or 8:1

d. Ratio of 540 to 48 = (540÷2)/(48÷2) = 270/24 = (270÷2)/(24÷2) = 135/12 = (135÷3)/(12÷3) = 45/4 or 45:4

**Example 3:** Write down the ratio of the first number to the second, in the simplest form.

a. 3/4 to 5/3
b. 2/13 to 15/16
c. 3.2 to 1.2
d. 7/9 to 0.7

**Solution:**
Using the formula a/b ÷ c/d = a/b × d/c = ad/bc

a. Ratio of 3/4 to 5/3 = (3/4)/(5/3) = (3/4) × (3/5) = 9/20 or 9:20

b. Ratio of 2/13 to 15/16 = (2/13)/(15/16) = (2/13) × (16/15) = 32/195 or 32:195

c. Ratio of 3.2 to 1.2 = 3.2/1.2 = (32/10) × (10/12) = 32/12 = (32÷4)/(12÷4) = 8/3 or 8:3

d. Ratio of 7/9 to 0.7 = (7/9)/(7/10) = (7/9) × (10/7) = 10/9 or 10:9

**Example 4:** Write the ratio of the first quantity to the second, in simplest terms:

a. 65 cents to 5 Birr
b. 12 meters to 200cm
c. 700 grams to 6kg
d. 600 seconds to 8 minutes

**Solution:**
First, make units the same.

a. 1 Birr = 500 cents
   The ratio of 65 cents to 5 Birr = Ratio of 65 cents to 500 cents
   = 65/500 = (65÷5)/(500÷5) = 13/100 or 13:100

b. 1m = 100cm, so 12m = 1200cm
   The ratio of 12m to 200cm = Ratio of 1200cm to 200cm
   = 1200/200 = 12/2 = 6/1 or 6:1

c. 1kg = 1000g, so 6kg = 6000g
   The ratio of 700g to 6kg = Ratio of 700g to 6000g
   = 700/6000 = 7/60 or 7:60

d. 1 minute = 60 seconds, so 8 minutes = 480 seconds
   The ratio of 600 seconds to 8 minutes = Ratio of 600 seconds to 480 seconds
   = 600/480 = (600÷12)/(480÷12) = 5/4 or 5:4

**Dividing quantities in a given ratio**

**Examples:**

**Solution:** Let the first part be 3x and the second part be 2x, then:
3x + 2x = 300
5x = 300
x = 60

Therefore:
First part = 3x = 3 × 60 = 180 Birr
Second part = 2x = 2 × 60 = 120 Birr

2. The ratio of two numbers is 7:3 and their sum is 50, then find the two numbers.

**Solution:** Let the first number be 7x and the second number be 3x, then:
7x + 3x = 50
10x = 50

Therefore:
First number = 7x = 7 × 5 = 35
Second number = 3x = 3 × 5 = 15

3. Two numbers have ratio 13:10. If their difference is 84, then find the larger number.

**Solution:** Let the larger number be 13x and the smaller number be 10x, then:
13x - 10x = 84
3x = 84
x = 28

Therefore, the larger number = 13 × 28 = 364

4. A bag contains red and white balls, the ratio of red to white balls is 3:4. If the bag contains 36 white balls, then find the number of red balls.

**Solution:** Let the number of red balls be 3x and white balls be 4x.
Number of white balls = 4x = 36
x = 9

Therefore, the number of red balls = 3x = 3 × 9 = 27

5. The ratio of the measures of the angles of a triangle is 1:2:3. Then find the measure of each angle.

**Solution:** Let the angles be x, 2x, and 3x.
x + 2x + 3x = 180°
6x = 180°
x = 30°

Therefore:
First angle = 1x = 1 × 30° = 30°
Second angle = 2x = 2 × 30° = 60°
Third angle = 3x = 3 × 30° = 90°

6. If a, b, and c are numbers such that a:b:c = 3:4:5 and b = 20, then find a + b + c.

**Solution:** a = 3x, b = 4x, c = 5x
b = 4x = 20
x = 5

Therefore:
a = 3x = 3 × 5 = 15
c = 5x = 5 × 5 = 25

**Definition 4.2:** Proportion is the equality of two ratios. If two ratios a:b and c:d are equal, we write a:b = c:d or a/b = c/d. In a proportion a:b = c:d:
- a and d are called extremes
- b and c are called means

**Property:** In any proportion, the product of means equals the product of extremes (i.e., if a/b = c/d, then a × d = b × c).

**Example 5:** Check whether the numbers 20, 45, 8 and 18 are in order of proportion.

**Solution:**
20/45 = 8/18
20 × 18 = 360
45 × 8 = 360

Since product of means equals product of extremes, the numbers 20, 45, 8 and 18 are in proportion.

**Example 6:** Check whether 3:5 = 6:12?

**Solution:**
Product of means = 5 × 6 = 30
Product of extremes = 3 × 12 = 36

Product of means ≠ product of extremes, hence the numbers 3, 5, 6 and 12 are not in proportion.

**Example 7:** If the numbers 3, 5, x and 20 are in order of proportion, then find the value of x.

**Solution:** 3:5 = x:20
Product of means = product of extremes
5x = 3 × 20
5x = 60
x = 12

**Example 8:** In proportion 3/5 = 9/y, find the value of y.

**Solution:** Using cross multiplication
3y = 5 × 9
3y = 45
y = 15

**Example 9:** The scale of a map is 1:1,000,000, then find the distance between two cities on a map if the actual distance between them is 75 km.

**Solution:**
Map scale = Map distance : Ground distance
1:1,000,000 = x:7,500,000 cm (since 75 km = 7,500,000 cm)
x = (1 × 7,500,000)/1,000,000 = 7.5 cm

Therefore, the distance on the map is 7.5 cm.

### Direct and Inverse Proportionality

#### A. Direct Proportionality

**Definition 4.3:** y is said to be directly proportional to x (written as y ∝ x), if there is a constant k such that y = kx or k = y/x. The number k is called the constant of proportionality.

If y ∝ x, then as x increases, y also increases or as x decreases, y also decreases. And the ratio y/x is constant.

**Example 8:** The following table shows the number of kilograms of sugar bought and the price paid in birr.

| Sugar in kg (x) | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 |
|-----------------|---|---|---|---|
| Price in Birr (y) | 30 | 60 | 90 | 120 |

a. Is y directly proportional to x? If so, what is the constant of proportionality?
b. Write the formula relating y and x.
c. Using the formula, calculate the price of 6.5 kg sugar.
d. How many kg of sugar will be bought with Birr 315?

**Solution:**
a. As x increases, y also increases and the ratio y/x is constant:
   k = 30/1 = 60/2 = 90/3 = 120/4 = 30, hence y ∝ x

b. y/x = k, implies y/x = 30 or y = 30x

c. y = ?, when x = 6.5 kg
   y = 30x = 30 × 6.5 = Birr 195

d. x = ?, when y = 315 Birr
   y = 30x
   315 = 30x
   x = 10.5 kg

**Example 9:** If y ∝ x and y = 48 when x = 12, find the value of m in the table:

| x | 12 | 6 |
|---|----|---|
| y | 48 | m |

**Solution:** Since y ∝ x, the ratio y/x is constant:
48/12 = m/6
4 = m/6
m = 24

**Example 10:** If 5 kg sweet potato costs Birr 120, then calculate the cost of 8 kg sweet potato.

**Solution:** As the amount increases, the cost increases. Hence cost is directly proportional to amount.
Cost of 8 kg = (120 × 8)/5 = Birr 192

#### B. Inverse Proportionality

**Definition 4.4:** y is said to be inversely proportional to x (written as y ∝ 1/x), if there is a constant k such that y = k/x or k = y × x. The number k is called the constant of proportionality.

If y ∝ 1/x, then as x increases, y decreases or as x decreases, y increases. And the product y × x is constant.

**Example 11:** The following table shows the time taken for a car to move a distance of 5000m at various speeds.

| Time in seconds (x) | 1000 | 500 | 250 | 200 |
|---------------------|------|-----|-----|-----|
| Speed in m/s (y)    | 5    | 10  | 20  | 25  |

a. Is y inversely proportional to x? If so, what is the constant of proportionality?
b. Write the formula relating y and x.
c. Using the formula, calculate the time taken by the car to cover the given distance moving at 8 m/s.

**Solution:**
a. As x increases, y decreases and the product y × x is constant:
   k = 5 × 1000 = 10 × 500 = 20 × 250 = 25 × 200 = 5000, hence y ∝ 1/x

b. y × x = k, implies y × x = 5000

c. x = ?, when y = 8 m/s
   y × x = 5000
   8 × x = 5000
   x = 625 seconds

**Example 12:** If y ∝ 1/x, find the value of n:

| x | 12 | 6 |
|---|----|---|
| y | 15 | n |

**Solution:** Since y ∝ 1/x, the product y × x is constant:
12 × 15 = 6 × n
180 = 6n
n = 30

**Example 13:** If 8 persons do a certain job in 9 hours, then how long will it take for 12 persons to do the same job? (Assume all persons work at the same rate).

**Solution:** As the number of persons increases, the time taken decreases. Hence, number of persons is inversely proportional to time taken.
Time taken by 12 persons = (8 × 9)/12 = 6 hours

**Converting Percent to Fraction**

**Example 1:** Convert 30% to fraction.

**Solution:**

3. Write the fraction in lowest terms

### 4.2.3 Calculating Percentage

**Activity 4.2.2: Discuss with your friends**

Calculate 10% of 200.

5% of 60 is calculated as follows:
5% of 60 = (5/100) × 60 = 300/100 = 3

In this calculation:
a. 5% is called **rate**
b. 60 is called **base**
c. 3 is called **percentage**

**Definition 4.6:**
- **Base (B)** is the number that represents 100% or total value of something.
- **Percentage (p)** is the number or amount that represents a part of the whole.
- **Rate (R)** is the ratio of percentage to base, written as percent.

**Example 8:** 20% of 150 is 30. Determine base, rate, and percentage.

**Solution:**
- The number expressed in percent is rate: **20% is rate**
- The whole part or number preceded by "of" is base: **150 is base**
- The part of whole is percentage: **30 is percentage**

**Formula relating Base, Rate, and Percentage:**

From the example, base, rate, and percentage are related by:
P = R × B

You can also use the triangle to remember:
```
    P
   / \
  R   B
```

**Example 9:** Calculate 5% of 120.

**Solution:**
P = R × B = 5% × 120 = (5/100) × 120 = 6

**Example 10:** Calculate:
a. 80% of Birr 260
b. 9.6% of 7.2 liters
c. 90% of 264 cm
d. 34% of 7000 kg

**Solution:**
a. P = 80% × 260 = (80/100) × 260 = 8 × 26 = Birr 208
b. P = 9.6% × 7.2 = (9.6/100) × 7.2 = 0.6912 liters
c. P = 90% × 264 = (90/100) × 264 = 237.6 cm
d. P = 34% × 7000 = (34/100) × 7000 = 2380 kg

**Example 11:** Eleni answered 80% of questions correctly in a mathematics test. If the test has 25 questions, how many did she answer correctly?

**Solution:**
R = 80%, B = 25, P = ?
P = R × B = 80% × 25 = (80/100) × 25 = 20

Therefore, she answered 20 questions correctly.

### 4.2.5 Finding Rate

**Example 14:** Calculate:

b. B = 2 kg = 2000 gm, P = 400 gm
   R = (400/2000) × 100% = 20%

   R = (30/60) × 100% = 50%

### 4.3.2 Profit and Loss

**Activity 4.3.2: Discuss with your friends**

1. A shopkeeper buys shoes for Birr 500 and sells for Birr 600. Find:

   b. Selling price

**Definition 4.7:**
- **Cost Price (C.P):** Price at which an article is purchased.
- **Selling Price (S.P):** Price at which an article is sold.
- **Profit (Gain):** When S.P > C.P, Profit = S.P - C.P
- **Loss:** When S.P < C.P, Loss = C.P - S.P

**Formulas:**
- Profit % = (Profit/C.P) × 100% = (S.P - C.P)/C.P × 100%
- Loss % = (Loss/C.P) × 100% = (C.P - S.P)/C.P × 100%

**Example 5:** A shopkeeper buys a jacket for Birr 500, cleans it, and sells for Birr 640. Calculate profit percent.

**Solution:**
C.P = 500, S.P = 640
Profit = 640 - 500 = 140
Profit % = (140/500) × 100% = 28%

**Example 6:** Girma bought 200 eggs at Birr 8 each. 20 eggs were damaged. He sold the remaining at Birr 8.5 each. Calculate profit/loss percent.

**Solution:**
C.P = 200 × 8 = Birr 1600
Eggs left = 200 - 20 = 180
S.P = 180 × 8.5 = Birr 1530
Loss = 1600 - 1530 = 70
Loss % = (70/1600) × 100% = 4.375%

**Example 7:** A dealer gained 10% profit by selling an article for Birr 330. What was the original price?

**Solution:**
Let C.P = x
S.P = 330
Profit % = 10%
(330 - x)/x = 10/100
330 - x = 0.1x
330 = 1.1x
x = Birr 300

### 4.3.3 Simple Interest

**Activity 4.3.3: Discuss with your friends**

1. If you deposit Birr 1000 in a bank:
   a. After a year it increases. Why?
   b. By what amount if the bank pays 10% per year?
   c. By what amount in 2 years at 10% per year?

2. Define in your own words:
   a. Interest
   b. Simple interest

**Definitions:**
- **Principal (P):** Money borrowed or invested
- **Interest (I):** Extra money paid for using the lender's money
- **Time (T):** Duration for which money is borrowed
- **Amount (A):** Total money including interest (A = P + I)
- **Rate (R):** Percentage charged, expressed as percent

**Simple Interest Formula:**
I = P × R × T

**Example 8:** Birr 2000 deposited at 7% per year for 1 year.
a. Principal = Birr 2000
b. Rate = 7% per year
c. Time = 1 year
d. Interest = 7% of 2000 = (7/100) × 2000 = Birr 140

**Example 9:** Birr 4000 deposited at 7% per year for 4 years.
a. I = PRT = 4000 × (7/100) × 4 = Birr 1120
b. A = P + I = 4000 + 1120 = Birr 5120

**Example 10:** Birr 700 borrowed at 12% per year for 3 months.
Solution: T = 3 months = 3/12 = 1/4 year
I = 700 × (12/100) × (1/4) = Birr 21

**Example 11:** Birr 1000 grows to 1200 in 4 years at simple interest. Find the rate.

**Solution:**
P = 1000, A = 1200, T = 4
I = A - P = 200
R = (I/(P×T)) × 100% = (200/(1000×4)) × 100% = 5% per year

### 4.3.4 Compound Interest

**Activity 4.3.4: Discuss with your friends**

1. Birr 1000 invested at 10% per year:
   a. Interest at end of first year
   b. Second year interest (on principal + first year's interest)
   c. Third year interest
   d. Total interest in 3 years

2. What is compound interest?

**Definition:** Compound interest is interest calculated on the initial principal plus accumulated interest from previous periods.

**Formula:** A = P(1 + R)^T and I = A - P

**Example 12:** Birr 2000 invested at 10% compounded annually for 3 years.

**Solution:**
Year 1: I₁ = 2000 × 10% = Birr 200
Year 2: P₂ = 2000 + 200 = 2200, I₂ = 2200 × 10% = Birr 220
Year 3: P₃ = 2200 + 220 = 2420, I₃ = 2420 × 10% = Birr 242

Total interest = 200 + 220 + 242 = Birr 662
Amount = 2000 + 662 = Birr 2662

Using formula:
A = 2000(1 + 0.1)³ = 2000 × 1.331 = Birr 2662
I = 2662 - 2000 = Birr 662

**Example 13:** What sum amounts to Birr 24,200 in 2 years at 10% compounded annually?

**Solution:**
A = P(1 + R)^T
24200 = P(1 + 0.1)²
24200 = P × 1.21
P = 24200/1.21 = Birr 20,000

### 4.3.5 VAT, Turnover Tax, and Income Tax

**Activity 4.3.5: Discuss with your friends**

1. Why do governments collect tax? List different types.

**Taxes** are imposed by governments to generate income for projects that boost the economy (administration, defense, education, housing, health, etc.).

#### Value Added Tax (VAT)

VAT is a tax on sales of goods and services.

**Note:**
- In Ethiopia, VAT rate is **15%**
- VAT amount = 15% of original cost
- Cost including VAT = original cost + VAT amount

**Example 16:** A machine costs Birr 3000 before VAT.
a. VAT = 15% of 3000 = (15/100) × 3000 = Birr 450
b. Total cost = 3000 + 450 = Birr 3450

**Example 17:** A TV stand costs Birr 2300 including VAT.
a. Cost before VAT = 2300/1.15 = Birr 2000
b. VAT amount = 2000 × 15% = Birr 300

#### Turn Over Tax (TOT)

TOT is imposed on merchants not registered for VAT.

**Note:**
- In Ethiopia:
  - Annual income below Birr 500,000 → registered for TOT
  - Annual income above Birr 500,000 → registered for VAT
- TOT rate is **2%** on goods sold and services rendered locally

**Example 18:** Daily sales of Birr 10,000.
TOT = 2% of 10,000 = (2/100) × 10,000 = Birr 200

**Example 19:** Elsa stationery has daily sales of Birr 205, open 250 days/year.
Annual sales = 205 × 250 = Birr 51,250
TOT = 2% of 51,250 = Birr 1,025

#### Employment Income Tax

Employers deduct tax based on the following rates (Ethiopian income tax rate):

| Employment Income (per month) in Birr | Tax Rate |
|---------------------------------------|----------|
| Above 0 up to 600                     | 0%       |
| Above 600 up to 1650                  | 10%      |
| Above 1650 up to 3200                 | 15%      |
| Above 3200 up to 5250                 | 20%      |
| Above 5250 up to 7800                 | 25%      |
| Above 7800 up to 10900                | 30%      |
| Over 10900                            | 35%      |

**Example 20:** Monthly salary of Birr 850.
- For 0-600: Tax₁ = 0
- For 600-850: Tax₂ = 10% of (850 - 600) = 10% × 250 = Birr 25
Income tax = 0 + 25 = **Birr 25**

**Example 21:** Monthly salary of Birr 4850.
- Tax₁ (0-600) = 0
- Tax₂ (600-1650) = 10% × 1050 = Birr 105
- Tax₃ (1650-3200) = 15% × 1550 = Birr 232.5
- Tax₄ (3200-4850) = 20% × 1650 = Birr 330
Income tax = 0 + 105 + 232.5 + 330 = **Birr 667.5**
Net income = 4850 - 667.5 = **Birr 4182.5**

**Example 22:** Find income tax on Birr x where x falls in interval 5250 to 7800.

**Solution:**
For 5250 < x ≤ 7800:
- Tax₁ (0-600) = 0
- Tax₂ (600-1650) = 10% × 1050 = Birr 105
- Tax₃ (1650-3200) = 15% × 1550 = Birr 232.5
- Tax₄ (3200-5250) = 20% × 2050 = Birr 410
- Tax₅ (5250-x) = 25% × (x - 5250) = 0.25x - 1312.5
Total tax = 0 + 105 + 232.5 + 410 + 0.25x - 1312.5 = **0.25x - 565**

## Summary for Unit 4

- **Ratio:** Method of comparing two or more quantities of the same kind and unit by division (a:b)
- **Proportion:** Equality of two ratios (a/b = c/d). Product of means = product of extremes (a × d = b × c)
- **Direct Proportion:** y ∝ x, y = kx, ratio y/x is constant
- **Inverse Proportion:** y ∝ 1/x, y = k/x, product y × x is constant
- **Percent:** "Per hundred" (%). 5% = 5/100
- **Base (B), Percentage (P), Rate (R):** P = R × B, B = P/R, R = (P/B) × 100%
- **Percent Change:**
  - Increase = (increase/original) × 100%
  - Decrease = (decrease/original) × 100%
- **Profit/Loss:**
  - Profit = S.P - C.P (when S.P > C.P)
  - Loss = C.P - S.P (when S.P < C.P)
  - Profit % = (Profit/C.P) × 100%
  - Loss % = (Loss/C.P) × 100%
- **Simple Interest:** I = PRT, A = P + I
- **Compound Interest:** A = P(1 + R)^T, I = A - P
- **VAT (Ethiopia):** 15% of original cost
- **Turnover Tax (Ethiopia):** 2% for merchants with annual income below Birr 500,000
- **Income Tax:** Progressive rates from 0% to 35%
