2. Say that:
   a. If AB = XY, BC = YZ and AC = XZ, then does △ABC cover △XYZ completely?
   b. What can you say about the two triangles?

If △ABC is congruent to △XYZ, then their corresponding parts must satisfy:
i. X falls on A, and ∠X ≅ ∠A
ii. Y falls on B, and ∠Y ≅ ∠B
iii. Z falls on C, and ∠Z ≅ ∠C
iv. XY falls along AB, and XY = AB
v. YZ falls along BC, and YZ = BC
vi. XZ falls along AC, and XZ = AC

**Definition 6.2:** Two triangles are congruent if their corresponding parts (angles and sides) that match one another are equal.

**Note:**
1. Two triangles are congruent if they are copies of each other and when you place one triangle on another, they cover each other completely.
2. If △ABC is congruent to △XYZ, then symbolically written as △ABC ≅ △XYZ.

**Example 1:** If △ABC ≅ △FED, then find the six congruent corresponding parts of the triangles.

**Solution:**
i. ∠A ≅ ∠F
iv. AB = FE
ii. ∠B ≅ ∠E
v. BC = ED
iii. ∠C ≅ ∠D
vi. AC = FD

**Example 2:** Consider the following triangles. Congruent angles are marked as indicated. Based on the figure, answer the following questions.

b. △QRP ≅ ?

**Solution:**

**Example 3:** Let △ABC ≅ △DEF, and m(∠A) = 70°, DE = 6cm. Then find m(∠D) and the length of AB.

**Solution:**
Since the triangles are congruent:
m(∠A) = m(∠D) = 70°
DE = AB = 6cm

#### 6.1.3 Tests for Congruence (SSS, SAS, ASA)

In this section, you will learn that you do not need all six pieces of information to show triangles are congruent. By using only three parts of a triangle and applying the three tests for congruence (SSS, SAS, ASA), you can show whether two triangles are congruent or not.

**A. Side-Side-Side (SSS) Congruence Test**

**Activity 6.1.3**

Read the following with your partners:

Given △ABC:
1. If only one side of △ABC is given (say 5.5cm), you cannot draw the exact copy of it. Can you draw an exact copy if only two sides are given (say 5.5cm and 3.4cm)?

The answer is NO, because you can draw a number of triangles using two sides and another arbitrary side as follows.

2. If only two sides of △ABC are given (say 5.5cm and 3.4cm), can you draw the exact copy of it?

The answer is NO, because you can draw a number of triangles using two sides and another arbitrary side as follows.

3. If all three sides of △ABC are given (AB = 5cm, BC = 5.5cm, AC = 3.4cm), can you draw the exact copy of it?

Yes! To draw an exact copy of △ABC, we need the lengths of three sides of the triangle. We call this the side-side-side congruence criterion.

**Side-Side-Side (SSS) Congruence Test:** If the three sides of one triangle are congruent to the three corresponding sides of another triangle, then the triangles are congruent.

**Example 4:** Examine whether the two triangles are congruent or not. If yes, write the congruence relation in symbolic form.

**Solution:**
AB = PR = 7.1cm
BC = PQ = 5cm
AC = QR = 3.5cm

The three sides of one triangle are equal to the three sides of the other triangle. So by SSS congruence test, the two triangles are congruent. You can easily see that A corresponds to R, B corresponds to P, and C corresponds to Q. Hence, △ABC ≅ △RPQ by SSS congruence test.

**Example 5:** Consider the following kite:
a. State the three pairs of equal parts in △ABD and △CBD.
b. Is △ABD ≅ △CBD? Why or why not?
c. Does BD bisect ∠ABC? Give reasons.

a. Three pairs of equal parts in △CBD are:
   AB = CB (given)
   AD = CD (given)
   BD = BD (common side for both)

b. From (a) above by SSS, △ABD ≅ △CBD.

c. From (b) above, ∠ABD ≅ ∠CBD (corresponding parts of congruent triangles). So, ∠ABC is bisected by BD.

**B. Side-Angle-Side (SAS) Congruence Test**

**Activity 6.1.4**

Read the following with your partners:

Given △ABC:
1. If only one side of △ABC is given (say 5.5cm), you cannot draw the exact copy as seen in Activity 5.3. Can you draw an exact copy if one side and one angle are given (say one side is 5.5cm and one angle is 65°)?

The answer is NO, because you can draw many different triangles using one side and one angle.

2. If two sides and the angle between these two sides is given (say BC = 5.5cm, AC = 3.4cm, and the angle between BC and AC is 65°), can you draw the exact copy of it?

In this case, you can draw a single triangle which is the exact copy of △ABC.

So, to draw an exact copy of △ABC, we need the lengths of two sides of the triangle and the angle between the two sides. We call this the side-angle-side congruence criterion.

**Side-Angle-Side (SAS) Congruence Test:** If two sides and the angle included between them of a triangle are equal to two corresponding sides and the angle included between them of another triangle, then the triangles are congruent.

**Example 6:** In the figure, AD bisects ∠A and AB = AC.
i. State the three pairs of equal parts in △ADB and △ADC
ii. Is △ADB ≅ △ADC? Give reason.
iii. Is ∠B = ∠C? Give reason.
iv. Is BD = DC? Give reason.

**Solution:**
i. Three pairs of equal parts:
   AB = AC (Given)
   AD = AD (Common side)
   m(∠BAD) = m(∠CAD) (AD bisects ∠BAC)

ii. Yes, △ADB ≅ △ADC (by SAS)

iii. Yes, ∠B = ∠C, because they are corresponding parts of congruent triangles.

iv. Yes, BD = DC, because they are corresponding parts of congruent triangles.

**C. Angle-Side-Angle (ASA) Congruence Test**

**Activity 6.1.5**

2. If only one angle and one side of a triangle are given, can you draw the exact copy of it?

**Angle-Side-Angle (ASA) Congruence Test:** If two angles and the included side of a triangle are equal to two corresponding angles and the included side of another triangle, then the triangles are congruent.

**Example 7:** △ABC ≅ △QRP by ASA, and BC = RP is given. What additional information is needed to establish ASA congruence test?

**Solution:** For ASA congruence test, we need the two angles between the two sides BC and RP. So the additional information is: ∠B = ∠R and ∠C = ∠P.

**Example 8:** Show that △AOC ≅ △BOD.

**Solution:**
In △AOC: m(∠A) + m(∠C) + m(∠O) = 180°
m(∠A) + 70° + 30° = 180°
m(∠A) = 80°

m(∠AOC) = m(∠DOC) = 30° (vertically opposite angles)

In △DOB: m(∠D) + m(∠O) + m(∠B) = 180°
70° + 30° + m(∠B) = 180°
m(∠B) = 80°

Now, AC = DB and the angles that include the sides AC and DB are also congruent (i.e., ∠A = ∠B, and ∠C = ∠D). So, by ASA, △AOC ≅ △BOD.

### 6.2 Applications

After completing this section, you will be able to:
- Apply congruency of plane figures to real-life problems.

In this section, you will learn some applications of congruence of triangles.

**Example 1:** Show that the diagonal of a rhombus bisects the angles at the vertex.

**Solution:** Let ABCD be a rhombus. We need to show AC bisects ∠BAD and ∠BCD.

Considering △ABC and △ADC:
AB = AD (All sides of rhombus are congruent)
BC = DC (All sides of rhombus are congruent)
AC = AC (Common side for both)

Therefore, △ABC ≅ △ADC by SSS congruence test.

∠BAC = ∠DAC (corresponding parts of congruent triangles), hence ∠BAD is bisected by AC.

∠BCA = ∠DCA (corresponding parts of congruent triangles), hence ∠BCD is bisected by AC.

**Example 2:** In the bridge structure shown, the length of cable AB = 5m, BC = 4m, EF = 4m, AC = 3m. What should be the length of cable DE? (Assume AC ⊥ BC and DF ⊥ FE)

**Solution:**
In △ABC and △DEF:
BC = EF = 4m (Given)
AC = DF = 3m (Given)
∠C = ∠F (Since AC ⊥ BC and DF ⊥ FE)

Therefore, △ABC ≅ △DEF by SAS congruence test, and DE = AB = 5m.

**Example 3:** A farmer wants to construct a fence. If BC = CE = 6m, ∠ABC = ∠CED, AE = 4m, and AC ⊥ BD, then calculate the length of:
a. CD
b. BD

**Solution:**
a. Consider △ABC and △CDE:
   BC = CE (Given)
   ∠ABC = ∠CED (Given)
   ∠ACB = ∠ECD (Since AC ⊥ BD)

   Therefore, △ABC ≅ △CDE by ASA.

   So, CD = AC = AE + EC = 4m + 6m = 10m

b. BD = BC + CD = 6m + 10m = 16m

**Example 4:** In the figure, ABCD is a rectangle and F, G, E, and H are the midpoints on sides AB, BC, CD, and AD respectively. Show that:
a. △FAH ≅ △FBG
b. △ECG ≅ △EDH
c. △FAH ≅ △EDH
d. HF = FG = EG = EH

**Solution:**
a. Consider △FAH and △FBG:
   FA = FB (Since F is midpoint of AB)
   AH = HD (Since AD = BC and H is midpoint of AD, G is midpoint of BC)
   m(∠A) = m(∠B) = 90° (Angles of rectangle)
   Therefore, △FAH ≅ △FBG by SAS.

b. Consider △ECG and △EDH:
   EC = ED (Since E is midpoint of CD)
   CG = HD (Since AD = BC and H is midpoint of AD, G is midpoint of BC)
   m(∠C) = m(∠D) = 90° (Angles of rectangle)
   Therefore, △ECG ≅ △EDH by SAS.

c. Consider △FAH and △EDH:
   FA = ED (Since AB = DC and F is midpoint of AB, E is midpoint of DC)
   AH = HD (Since H is midpoint of AD)
   m(∠A) = m(∠D) = 90° (Angles of rectangle)
   Therefore, △FAH ≅ △EDH by SAS.

d. From (a), (b), and (c) you can see that △FAH ≅ △FBG ≅ △ECG ≅ △EDH. Hence, HF = GF = GE = HE since the corresponding sides of congruent triangles are equal.

## Summary for Unit 6

**Key Concepts:**

- Two figures are congruent if one figure covers the other completely and exactly.
- Two line segments are congruent if they have the same length.
- Two angles are congruent if they have the same measure.
- Two triangles are congruent if their corresponding parts (angles and sides) that match one another are equal.
- If △ABC ≅ △XYZ, then the corresponding parts are equal: ∠A ≅ ∠X, ∠B ≅ ∠Y, ∠C ≅ ∠Z, AB = XY, BC = YZ, AC = XZ.

**Congruence Tests:**
1. **SSS (Side-Side-Side):** If three sides of one triangle are congruent to three corresponding sides of another triangle, the triangles are congruent.
2. **SAS (Side-Angle-Side):** If two sides and the included angle of one triangle equal two corresponding sides and included angle of another triangle, the triangles are congruent.
3. **ASA (Angle-Side-Angle):** If two angles and the included side of one triangle equal two corresponding angles and included side of another triangle, the triangles are congruent.
